# The Pressure Campaign

*Momentum Acquisitions vs. Rainier Lanes*
*All tactics legal. All designed to exhaust rather than win.*

---

## Structural Pressure

*These run in the background. They don't have a specific moment the players can intervene in — they're the texture of the situation. Marla mentions them in Act 1. They explain why she's exhausted.*

---

**Three Purchase Offers**
First below market, second at market, third above market. All declined. Momentum's internal read: irrational seller. The correct read: she's not selling at any price. These are over — they've moved past offers.

**Noise Complaint Campaign**
A rolling series of complaints filed by neighboring addresses, some real tenants Momentum has relationships with, some filed more creatively. No single complaint is actionable. The point is the accumulation — a paper record that the alley is a community nuisance — and the time Marla has to spend responding to each one in writing. Kyle manages the filing schedule so they arrive just frequently enough to be exhausting without being obviously coordinated.

**Community Outreach — Capitol Rise**
Momentum has been circulating a slick one-pager in the neighborhood: local jobs, improved walkability, new housing, street-level retail. It does not mention that Rainier Lanes would be demolished to build it. Some residents are enthusiastic. The one-pager has appeared in Rainier Lanes' mailbox twice.

**Insurance Flagging**
Momentum flagged the property to a commercial insurance broker contact as being in an active acquisition dispute. Marla's next renewal will likely come back with a rate increase or additional requirements. She doesn't know why yet. She won't be able to trace it back.

**Letter to Her Father's Address**
Kyle ran the original business registration and sent a letter to the father's last known address as a routine angle — a different signatory might be more persuadable. It came back undelivered. He noted it and moved on. He has no idea why the address is dead. Marla will find this unsettling if the players surface it: she knows exactly why, and the idea of Momentum even trying to reach him bothers her more than she shows.

## Active Pressure

*Two things with a specific moment. A window. Something the players can act on. Resolving both is the cap of Act 2 — once these close, Momentum has exhausted their institutional angles and the session moves into Act 3.*

---

**Building Inspection — Second Team** `upcoming`
The first inspection found nothing — the building is in better shape than it has any right to be. Kyle has identified a contact who can ensure the inspector assigned to the second visit is thorough. This one focuses on electrical and egress, where older buildings have more exposure. It's scheduled, and it's coming.

*What the players can do:* Several angles. Get ahead of it — commission an independent electrical and egress assessment before the inspection date so there's a clean report already on record. Dig into who requested it and surface that it was filed by a party with a financial interest in the outcome, which poisons its credibility. Or find the city contact Kyle is using and make clear that someone is watching. Any of these works. The goal is that the inspector finds nothing actionable and Momentum loses this angle.

*Resolution:* Inspection concludes with no violations. Momentum's administrative avenue closes.

---

**The Neighbor Complaints** `active`
Momentum approached a handful of immediate neighbors with a reasonable-sounding pitch: the parking lot gets loud in the evenings, people hanging around, music from inside, it goes late sometimes. The neighbors aren't invested — they were approached at the right moment with a plausible grievance and said sure, fine. Their noise complaints are now part of the administrative record. Without them, the proceedings have no standing.

Solomon knows who filed. He was approached the same way and refused. He's been watching the same neighbors, and he's pretty sure most of them feel awkward about it now.

*What the players can do:* Organize a parking lot evening — grill, music, open doors, invite the block. The same neighbors who filed show up, spend a couple of hours making friendly noise in the very spot they complained about, and remember that this is their space too. No confrontation needed. Just presence.

The irony is the point: the complaint was noise in the parking lot. The solution is noise in the parking lot, with everyone invited. Once a neighbor has eaten free food there and talked to Marla, filing a complaint against her feels different.

The Satyr and the Pooka are basically built for this scene. Solomon handles the food side if asked. Marla stays inside and pretends not to know what they're doing, because if she organizes it herself it looks desperate.

*Resolution:* Enough neighbors withdraw that the proceedings collapse. Momentum loses the paper record they were building.

## Context the Players Can Uncover

*Not things to act on — things that reframe the situation. Useful for Act 2 investigation.*

**The Capitol Rise Development Plan** — publicly filed with the city, searchable. Full footprint: the entire block face — Rainier Lanes, parking lot, Solomon's, Earl's barbershop. Seven stories, fifty units, ground-floor retail. Q3 projected timeline. None of the three owners know the full scope of what's filed. Finding this and showing it to any of them is a scene. It reframes everything: Momentum isn't making opportunistic offers, they're executing a funded project. The offers were never going to be the end of it regardless of who said yes.

**Momentum's Pattern** — two previous acquisitions, same playbook. One owner later said they felt they had no choice. The other property sold after a minor incident that was never conclusively explained. Useful if the players want to make noise publicly — a local journalist or neighborhood council meeting with named documentation raises the cost for Momentum without stopping them.

**The Letter to Her Father** — Kyle sent a letter to the father's last known address as a routine angle. Returned undelivered. If players surface this, Marla's reaction is telling. She knows exactly why the address is dead. The idea of Momentum even trying to reach him unsettles her more than she shows.

---

## What Marla Has Told the Party

At the start of the session: the offers, the inspections, the noise complaints, the parking proceedings. She knows it's coordinated because Solomon told her about his approach. She hasn't told them about the Capitol Rise plan because she doesn't know it exists. She hasn't told them about the letter to her father's address because it's personal.

She's been managing this alone for months. She was hoping she could handle it. She is running out of room.
