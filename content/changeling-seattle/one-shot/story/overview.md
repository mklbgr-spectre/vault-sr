**Purpose:** Standalone system demo. Introduces Changeling mechanics and tone to a Shadowrun group. No continuity with the campaign that follows.

**Adventure model:** Banal threat — a developer is applying sustained pressure on a community space. The threat is mundane, grinding, and institutional. Most accessible for Shadowrun veterans.

---

## Premise

Inspired loosely by *Batteries Not Included* — a storied but aging community space under outside pressure, quietly kept alive by a creature from the Dreaming that only one person knows about.

**The space:** Rainier Lanes. An old bowling alley on the South Capitol Hill / CD border. Full of interesting backspaces — pin machinery, ball returns, maintenance crawlways, decades of accumulated storage. Home to a Krofted Glome anchored to the building's original foundations and machinery. See `locations/rainier-lanes.md`.

**The creature:** The Glome has been quietly maintaining the building for decades — the reason it hasn't fallen apart. Patient, hidden, not aggressive. Its Anchor *is* the building; if the building is destroyed, so is it. See `glome.md`.

**Marla:** Owner-in-trust. Grew up in this building. Her father — full kith Eshu — left on a quest two years ago and hasn't returned. She knows about the Glome. She has kept that secret from everyone, including the kith regulars. She is being systematically pressured by a developer who wants the property. She is running out of room. See `characters/kinain.md`.

**The developer:** Momentum Acquisitions. Bryce Harrington. The threat is institutional — lawyers, inspectors, manufactured complaints. Not a monster. A process. See `antagonists.md` and `pressure.md`.

**The players:** Regulars. People who belong to this space. They don't know about the Glome. They don't know the full scope of what Marla is dealing with. They're about to find out both.
