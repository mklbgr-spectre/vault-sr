## Who She Is

Marla grew up behind the counter at Rainier Lanes. Her father — full kith, Eshu — ran the place and made no secret of the world. She understood what the regulars were before she understood most things about herself. The bowling alley was neutral ground because he made it so; she inherited the policy along with the keys.

Two years ago he left on a quest, sworn to a lord. She doesn't know the full terms, doesn't know the timeline, doesn't know if or when he comes back. She knows that quests are serious and that arguing with one is pointless. He handed her the responsibility and trusted her to hold it. That's what she's doing.

She is the steward, not the owner. She feels this distinction and doesn't talk about it.

**The Glome:** She's known about it since childhood — her father showed her, or she found it and he confirmed it. The Glome knew her as a child; its trust in her is partly inherited, partly earned in the two years she's been holding things together alone. She knows the building and the creature are the same thing. She will not sell because she cannot sell. It's not hers to sell.

**The developer pressure** hits her differently because of all this. She can't call her dad. She's making calls he should be making, on a timeline that wasn't her choice, protecting something that isn't fully hers yet. She's doing it anyway.

---

## Appearance

Warm medium-brown skin, dark straight hair usually pulled back when working. The kind of face that looks younger than 31 until she's tired, and then looks exactly 31. She's been behind a counter her whole life — she has the posture of someone who is always slightly watching the room without appearing to watch the room.

Practical clothes. Usually has a dish towel tucked into her waistband. Moves through the space the way people do when they've memorized every inch of a building.

## The Drink

Marla mixes a house concoction — regulars have their own name for it. It clears your head, settles something, makes you feel like yourself again. She learned the recipe from her father. She doesn't know it's Awaken. She just knows it works.

*Mechanically: Spring 1, Awaken. Roll Glamour (pool 2) at difficulty 6. One success is sufficient to trigger the effect.*

## Notes for Play

- Not an adventurer. No combat role, no reason to be in danger.
- She's the reason the party cares before anything supernatural happens. Establish that early.
- If asked about her father: she answers briefly and changes the subject. It's not that she's hiding it — it's that there's nothing useful to say.
