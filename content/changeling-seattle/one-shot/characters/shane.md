## Cantrips

**Hopscotch** (Wayfare 1)
Caster or target leaps extraordinary distances. 1 success = 1 story up / 30ft broad; up to "as far as the eye can see" at 5. Landing is always safe regardless of fall distance. Objects leap wildly if targeted — anyone in contact can resist with Str + Athletics (diff 7). Type: Wyrd.

**Quicksilver** (Wayfare 2)
Each success grants one extra action or doubles movement on the next turn. Split successes between the two as desired. Hurled objects add successes as bonus damage dice on a hit. Type: Wyrd.

**Sparrows and Nightingales** (Metamorphosis 1)
Change one physical feature of target — height, voice, hair, gender, object material. Must be a plausible alternative. Duration by successes: 1=scene, 2=day, 3=Glamour in days, 4=month, 5=year. Type: Chimerical or Wyrd.

## Mortal Identity

Pankration instructor — runs a small dojo off 12th Ave, Capitol Hill. Six students, no profit, all intention. Pankration is ancient Greek full-contact combat, no rules, everything a weapon. Niche enough to turn heads; serious enough that nobody questions her credentials after watching her move.

---

## Play Notes

Physical powerhouse and the group's conscience in combat. High Brawl and the frailty are a natural combination — she gets pulled into situations and then commits fully. The dojo is her equivalent of Marla's alley: her space, her terms, her livelihood. That ownership is the emotional bridge between her and this story.

Wayfare makes her uniquely mobile in the Act 3 fight — Hopscotch to close distance or escape, Quicksilver to overwhelm. Metamorphosis secondary is situational but memorable when it lands.

The player doesn't need to perform passion — just play someone who cares about things too much and acts on it.
