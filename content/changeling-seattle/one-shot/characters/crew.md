# The Crew

Three local troublemakers. First appear at the grill evening (Act 2) as ambient friction; hired by Kyle for the Act 3 break-in after he hears about them through the neighborhood grapevine.

They are not professionals. They said yes because they needed the money and wanted the action. They were not prepared for what the building contains.

**Alternate names provided** — if these three are logically unavailable in Act 3, swap in the alternates and use the same stat blocks for whoever Kyle sends instead.

---

## Demarco / *alternate: Andre*

Late 20s. Black. The one who talks first and assumes that means he's in charge. Usually right.

**Personality:** Performs confidence, always running exit math in the background. Not stupid — he stops being stupid when the stakes get real. At the grill evening he's testing, probing, looking for a reaction. In Act 3 he's the first to say *we need to go* once the building starts fighting back.

## Tomas / *alternate: Marcus*

Late 20s. Latino. Doesn't talk. Stands there. That's the threat.

**Personality:** Loyal to Demarco the way a dog is loyal to whoever fed it — without much analysis. Has a line he won't cross that he hasn't located yet. In Act 3 he's the last one to panic and the hardest to put down, but when Demarco says run, he runs.

## Cody / *alternate: Tyler*

Early 20s. White. Youngest. Wants to be here more than the other two. That's the problem.

**Personality:** Still auditioning for the crew. Will escalate when he shouldn't to prove a point to nobody in particular. At the grill evening he's the one who says something stupid. In Act 3 he's the first to bolt — not because he's the smartest, but because he's the most scared once the building starts moving.

## Notes for Play

- **Demarco** is who the party negotiates with at the grill evening. Responds to strength or a better offer, not appeals to conscience.
- **Tomas** is not a talker. Pointing him at a problem is Kyle's whole use for him.
- **Cody's** Willpower 2 makes him the easiest cantrip target and the easiest to spook. If you need one of them to break first, it's him.
- In Act 3 they flee as a unit once Demarco calls it. He calls it when either Tomas is down or the building does something that has no explanation.
- **Banality and the Mists:** At 5–6, the Mists take days rather than hours. They'll carry hazy to vivid memories for up to a week — useless as testimony, unsettling as personal experience. What Kyle gets back is confused fragments: something about a machine, the building moving, nobody got a clear look at anything.
