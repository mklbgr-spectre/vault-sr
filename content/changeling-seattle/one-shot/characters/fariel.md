## Cantrips

**Trick of the Light** (Chicanery 1)
Viewers misinterpret target for 1 turn per success — wrong face, wrong voice, wrong object. Doesn't hold up to scrutiny; anyone paying close attention for more than a turn can roll Perception + Occult (diff 8) to see through it. Type: Chimerical.

**Veiled Eyes** (Chicanery 2)
Target becomes instantly forgettable — not invisible, just not worth noticing. Duration by successes: 1=turn, 2=minute, 3=hour, 4=scene, 5=day. Additional Realms can create exceptions (e.g. Fae to allow fae to still perceive the target). Resisted by Perception + Kenning (diff 8) if actively looking. Type: Chimerical or Wyrd.

**Omen** (Soothsay 1)
A brief, vague glimpse at the thread of a person, place, or thing's destiny. Always subject to interpretation. By successes: 1=clue about immediate plans, 2=outward nature/court, 3=long-term goal, 4=inner nature, 5=closely-guarded secret. Repeated castings on the same target within a story cost +1 Glamour each. Type: Chimerical.

## Kith: Piskie

**Boon — Wings:** In fae mien, Piskies have wings and can fly at running speed. Also supernaturally nimble — add Glamour rating to Dexterity rolls to squeeze through tight spaces, escape holds, or move through obstacles.

**Frailty — Compulsive Curiosity:** Cannot ignore an unsolved puzzle, mystery, or riddle without a Willpower roll (difficulty 7). This includes people who don't add up.

## Mortal Identity

Freelance journalist — Stranger, alt-press, occasional Seattle Times bylines. Covers the "weird Seattle" beat: zoning disputes that don't make sense, neighborhoods that change overnight, businesses that shouldn't still be open but are. His instincts are better than his notes, and his notes are very good.

---

## Play Notes

The face and the finder. High social stats plus Investigation 3 makes him the party's primary information tool in Acts 1 and 2. Chicanery lets him hide things and hide people; Soothsay lets him feel the edges of what's actually going on before anyone else does.

The Chrysalis-as-amnesia framing means he has an in-world reason to ask questions and react with fresh eyes — lean into that as a system introduction tool. The player can ask "wait, what is that?" and it's Fariel asking, not the player.

Banality 4 is the highest in the group — he's most embedded in the mortal world, most at risk from Banality triggers. That's a meaningful mechanical distinction in Act 3.

## Seattle Backstory Hooks

- Freelance journalist — Stranger and alt-press bylines, the "weird Seattle" beat: zoning fights, disappearing storefronts, things that shouldn't still exist but do
- Has been working the Capitol Rise story for three months; something about the filings doesn't add up and he can't stop pulling threads
- Started coming to Rainier Lanes while reporting on the corridor. Stayed because the place remembered him even when he couldn't quite remember it.
- Has gaps he's stopped explaining away. Some of what he knows, he doesn't know how he knows. This used to bother him.
