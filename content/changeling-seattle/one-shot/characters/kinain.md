## Concept

Owner of a bowling alley in Seattle — inherited through [family heirloom OR late spouse, TBD]. She knows what she is. She did not choose to become the informal gathering point for non-court, non-freehold kith in the area; it happened around her, and she has made her peace with it.

The space runs as neutral ground because the kith who use it want it that way and self-police accordingly. Marla doesn't enforce it. Her Eshu lineage means there's something crossroads-like about her presence — travelers find her, things settle there that need settling — but the flavor is local, Pacific Northwest, grounded. No mythology on the surface. She's just a woman who owns a bowling alley and knows more than she lets on.

**She knows about the Glome.** Has known for some time. She has kept this secret from everyone — including the kith regulars. The Glome is bonded to the building's foundations and original machinery; it has been quietly maintaining the place for decades. Marla understands, at least instinctively, that the building and the creature are the same thing. She will not sell. She is not considering it.

**She is being harassed.** A developer wants the property. The campaign has been sustained and escalating — lawyers, manufactured code violations, inspector visits, noise complaints, personal pressure. She is running out of room to maneuver and is starting to consider telling the party the truth, or at least part of it.

The party are regulars. Relationships range from fleeting (she knows your face and your usual lane) to genuine acquaintance (she knows when something's wrong before you do). How warm that is depends on how much any given character has bothered to be chatty over the assumed shared history.

**No Egyptian flavor** — that is reserved for the full campaign Eshu character.

## Kinain Notes

**Awareness:** Knows her nature fully — no discovery arc for Marla.

**Neutral ground:** The bowling alley holds as neutral because the kith who use it want it to. She facilitates by existing, not by managing. The Eshu crossroads quality does the work passively.

**The drink:** Marla mixes a house concoction — regulars have their own name for it. It clears your head, settles something, makes you feel like yourself again. She doesn't know it's Awaken. She just knows the recipe works. It does.

**Visibility:** Her Eshu lineage is perceptible to any changeling with sufficient Kenning — it's not hidden, and over time regulars simply know. This is part of what makes the space work: she's a known quantity. Not fae, no court, no agenda, but not blind either. The community trusts the lanes partly because they know exactly what Marla is.

## Play Notes

GM character — present to anchor the scene, provide connections, and give the party somewhere to land. Not an adventurer. Has skin in the game because the bowling alley is the game.

Mortal enough to be grounded, fae-aware enough to be useful. Keep her warm but not soft — she's seen enough to not be easily rattled. The party being regulars means she reads them; she notices when something is off before they've said a word.
