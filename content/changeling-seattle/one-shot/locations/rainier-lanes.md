# Rainier Lanes

*South Capitol Hill / Central District border, Seattle*
*Est. 1961*

---

## The Block

The alley sits on the southern edge of Capitol Hill where it bleeds into the Central District. The CD is Seattle's historically Black neighborhood and the gentrification pressure here is documented and ongoing — tech money, Amazon expansion, longtime residents pushed out. A 1961 bowling alley on this block is exactly the kind of thing that shouldn't still exist. The developer knows this.

**Immediate surroundings:**
- **South:** Surface parking lot — the developer's actual target. A lot this size on this block is worth more than the building.
- **North:** Solomon's Ethiopian restaurant, twenty years on this block. Solomon knew Marla's father, refused Momentum's offer, told Marla immediately. He knows he's next if she goes.
- **Corner:** Earl's barbershop. Been there since before the bowling alley. Earl received Momentum's offer, set it aside, and hasn't thought much about it since. He's seen developers before.
- **Across the street:** Four-story glass-and-steel condo building, finished eighteen months ago. Ground-floor artisanal coffee. Half the units still empty.
- **One block east:** Luxury apartment tower under construction. The crane is visible from the parking lot.

All three businesses on this block face — Marla, Solomon, Earl — have received offers from Momentum. They are each at a different stage of understanding what that means.

The contrast is visible from the front door. The Rainier Lanes sign is original neon — the "R" flickers. The building across the street has a backlit LED panel that never flickers. This is what the neighborhood looks like right now.

---

## The Building

Eight lanes. A rental counter. A small snack bar. The wood is worn but solid — the Glome sees to that. Nothing has been renovated because nothing has needed to be.

**Layout (west to east):**
- Entry / shoe rental counter
- Eight lanes (1–8; lane 7 sits directly above the main maintenance shaft)
- Snack bar at the far end
- Back room behind the snack bar: two pool tables, unofficial kith gathering space — no sign, no announcement, it just is
- Door marked **Staff Only** off the back room: maintenance crawlways, storage, the original pin machinery. The Glome's domain.

**The maintenance crawlways:** Low, tight, lit by single bulbs on pull-strings. The original 1961 pin-setting machinery is back here — technically functional, never removed when the alley modernized. The Glome's Anchor.

## Atmosphere

The alley smells like lane oil and old wood and something underneath both — something Kenning-sensitive characters will notice as Glamour-dense before they can name why. This building has been a gathering point for a long time. It accumulates.

Regulars know the space with their bodies: which floorboard squeaks, which lane runs slightly left, which stool at the snack bar wobbles. That kind of comfort is built up over years of returning to the same place.

The back room works as community space because the community decided it does. Marla facilitates by existing, not by managing. Kith self-police. It works.

---

## As a Freehold

Rainier Lanes is not formally designated as a Freehold — no oaths, no court claims. But it functions as one. The Glome's presence anchors it; Marla's Kinain nature makes it a natural crossroads; decades of Glamour-active regulars have left their mark. Any changeling with Kenning will feel the difference between this place and the street outside.

The developer cannot feel this. That is the entire problem.

---

## The Developer's Play

The parking lot is the target. The building is the obstacle. The approach has been sustained and institutional: manufactured code violations, noise complaints, inspector visits on inconvenient schedules, legal letters designed to wear down rather than win. All mundane. All legal. All calibrated so Marla runs out of room before they run out of patience.

The developer does not know about the Glome. Does not know about Kithain. Just knows this property won't move and has decided to make it hurt until it does.
