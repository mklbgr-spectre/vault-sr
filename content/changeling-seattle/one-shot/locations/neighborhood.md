# The Block — Neighborhood POIs

*South Capitol Hill / Central District border*
*The immediate surroundings of Rainier Lanes*

---

## Solomon's Ethiopian Restaurant

**North of Rainier Lanes. Two storefronts wide, twenty years on this block.**

Solomon Tesfaye opened this place when the CD was a different neighborhood — when you could afford the rent and count on the regulars showing up. He still can, but the math is getting harder. He makes injera from scratch every morning. The lunch crowd is loyal; the dinner crowd is thinning as the neighborhood changes around him.

He knew Marla's father. They were never close friends — more like neighboring trees in the same soil, aware of each other's roots. When Momentum's letter arrived, he refused immediately, then walked over and told Marla the same day. He didn't ask what she was going to do. He just thought she should know.

Solomon is watching the same neighbors she is. He has a strong idea who on the block has been talking to Momentum. He won't name names without being asked directly, and even then he'll frame it carefully — he has to keep living here.

**In play:** The party's most reliable adult in the room. He won't pretend things are better than they are, but he won't catastrophize either. He's been watching developers circle this block for fifteen years. He's still here.

---

## Earl's Barbershop

**Corner of the block. Here since before the bowling alley.**

Earl Watkins has been cutting hair on this corner since 1958 — three years before Rainier Lanes was built. He's outlasted two recessions, a fire in the building next door, and more development cycles than he bothers to count. The shop is small, the chairs are vintage, and the waiting area has a rotating cast of men who have nowhere better to be and aren't looking.

Momentum's letter came. He set it aside on the counter under a coffee mug, and it's been there since. He hasn't thought much about it, which is different from not having noticed it. Earl has a long view on things. He's seen people show up with money and promises before. Most of the time they move on when something easier comes along.

He's not an activist. He's not a cynic. He's a man who has been somewhere a long time and intends to keep being there.

**In play:** Unhurried, warm, useful for context. If the party wants to know what the block looked like fifteen years ago, or twenty, or forty — Earl remembers. He won't be mobilized by urgency, but he responds to respect.

---

## New Condo Building

**Directly across the street. Finished eighteen months ago.**

Seven stories, glass and steel, a facade that photographs well and feels like nothing in person. The ground floor is a coffee concept — exposed concrete, reclaimed wood, oat milk, prices that don't quite make sense for the neighborhood but make complete sense for who the building is hoping to attract.

Half the units are still empty. The developer's projections assumed the neighborhood would shift faster than it has. The coffee shop is doing better than expected, which tells you something about the foot traffic, and worse than it looks, which tells you something about the margins.

Nobody in this building has lived here long enough to know what was here before.

**In play:** Visual shorthand for what Momentum wants the whole block to look like. The Rainier Lanes sign flickers across the street from it. That contrast is visible from the front door of either building.

---

## Surface Parking Lot

**South of Rainier Lanes. Owned separately, leased for monthly parking.**

A full block of surface parking in this location is, according to people who think about these things, worth significantly more than the building next to it. Combined — the lot and the alley — the math for a seven-story mixed-use development becomes straightforward.

The lot operator has already been approached. He said yes in principle, contingent on the adjacent property. He is waiting.

**In play:** Not a character, but worth pointing at. The party can see the crane from the lot. The contrast between what's here now and what Momentum is building one block east is the clearest possible statement of intent.

---

## Capitol Rise Construction Site

**One block east. Tower under construction, crane visible from the parking lot.**

This is where Momentum's last project is going up. Bryce Harrington's current investors have money in this one too. It's ahead of schedule — Q3 is looking realistic — and the sales office on the ground floor has a rendering of what the neighborhood will look like when it's done. Rainier Lanes is not in the rendering.

No one lives here yet. There's a night security guard who drinks coffee from a thermos and doesn't get paid enough to care about much.

**In play:** The Q3 deadline. This is why the pressure on Marla is accelerating — the same investors are watching both projects. If Capitol Rise sells well, the Rainier Lanes acquisition needs to be moving. If it stalls, Momentum needs a win somewhere else. Either way, the clock is ticking.
