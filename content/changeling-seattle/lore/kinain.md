# Kinain

Mortals with fae blood somewhere in their ancestry. Close enough to manifest in tangible ways, not enough to trigger a full Chrysalis. A permanent in-between state — adjacent to the fae world but never fully inside it. They do not Chrysalis.

## What They Can Do

- Perceive the fae world to varying degrees — see through the Mists more easily, won't forget fae encounters
- At stronger blood levels: perceive chimera, see through Glamour disguises, recognize Kithain on sight
- Some can use limited Arts (one or two dots in a single Art reflecting ancestry) with a very limited Glamour pool
- Generate Glamour like Dreamers

## What They Can't Do

- Enter the Dreaming freely — Trods are generally closed to them without assistance
- No fae mien
- Don't accumulate Banality the same way mortals do, but are more vulnerable to Glamour's wilder effects

## Social Position

Varies enormously. At best: valued protected community members; noble houses deliberately cultivate Kinain bloodlines. At worst: treated as lesser beings belonging to neither world.

**House Eiluned** tends to view Kinain with genuine interest — their in-between nature aligns with Eiluned's fascination with hidden things and boundaries.

## In Play (One-Shot)

A Kinain face character works well as a viewpoint/exposition anchor: experiences the fae world with fresh eyes, provides organic exposition, uses genuinely mundane social skills rather than cantrip shortcuts.

Key decision for the one-shot: does this character already know what they are, or does the session serve as their moment of realization?
