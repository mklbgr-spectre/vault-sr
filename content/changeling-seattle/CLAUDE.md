## What This Project Is

A campaign design workspace for a **Changeling: The Dreaming** campaign set in Seattle. The players are a Shadowrun 5 group trying a new system — no story, setting, or characters carry over. The one-shot is a standalone system demo using translated Shadowrun characters as pregens; everything after that is a fresh start.

The planned format is an **Obsidian vault** — interconnected markdown notes organized for GM reference during play.

Future work involves expanding notes into a full vault structure. System-level reference material (Kinain, kith mechanics, etc.) lives in `lore/`.

## Locked Design Decisions

These are settled — do not propose reversals:

- **No metaplot.** Episodic structure, persistent background pressures only. GM-rotation friendly.
- **Clean one-shot / campaign separation.** The one-shot is a self-contained system demo. The campaign that follows is a complete fresh start — new characters, no continuity with the one-shot or with Shadowrun.
- **Banal threat model as primary adventure structure.** Keeps the mortal toolkit active and is most accessible for Shadowrun veterans.
- **Central concept: band + venue = Freehold.** Music as Glamour engine, gigs as "the run is tonight," venue as the thing worth protecting.
- **Thematic north star:** *Can a community hold onto what makes it special against forces that want to flatten it?* — Gentrification as Banality.

## Campaign Setting Anchors

- **Kingdom of Pacifica** — less sourcebook coverage = more creative latitude
- **Capitol Hill / Seattle Underground / Pike Place Market** — primary Glamour-rich locations
- **Tech campuses (Amazon, Microsoft, Eastside)** — Banality-as-antagonist, not combat threats
- **Shadowrun bridge:** Megacorps map to Banality; Salish-Shidhe ("Shidhe" is intentional) parallels fae presence

## Recommended Sourcebooks

- *Freeholds & Hidden Glens* — location writeups that function as adventure seeds
- *Kithbook* series — community-level conflicts as natural adventure seeds
- *The Fool's Luck: The Way of the Commoner* — reframes the game from non-noble perspective; useful for a band/scrappy-gang concept
- *Pour L'Amour et Liberte* — most complete published adventure (New Orleans court intrigue); good structural reference
- *Immortal Eyes* trilogy — ambitious full campaign; linear but respected

## Tone and Framing Notes

- Banality arrives as mundane forces (rent increases, inspectors, chains replacing independents), not obvious monsters
- Success is not permanent victory — it is *holding the line long enough for something to take root*
- Mortal life has genuine stakes that fae powers do not resolve; casual cantrip use has costs (Banality accumulation, frailties)
- The group doesn't get credit — the neighborhood just keeps breathing when it probably shouldn't
