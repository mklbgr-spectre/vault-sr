## Project Overview

This is a **Shadowrun tabletop RPG campaign** set in London, using the **Shadowrun Anarchy** ruleset. The "codebase" is campaign documentation — world-building, character sheets, and setting lore. There are no build commands, tests, or scripts.

## Campaign Essentials

**The Organization:** Players are Squire-rank members of the **Knights of the Chalice** — a secretive magical security order operating as supernatural investigators (think magical MI7). Emphasis on discretion, merit, and reputation over typical shadowrunner violence.

**System:** Shadowrun Anarchy — lightweight, narrative-focused. Rules questions should reference the PDF. Avoid SR5 mechanics.

**Advancement:** Merit Points (narrative rewards) and Cyber Favor Points (spent to request hacking support from the Order's decker).

**Current Adventure:** "The Harrowgate Incident" — a failed spirit summoning at Harrowgate Hall (magical academy) that students attempted to cover up. PCs must identify the summoners and neutralize the spirit.

## Key Factions & Locations

**Magical Institutions:** Hermetic College of Oxford (formal/academic), Avalon Institute (druidic), Thames Academy (corporate technomagic), Westminster Academy (enchantment/politics)

**Corporate Players:** Saeder-Krupp (dominant), Horizon, NeoNET — all with magical arms and influence over London's institutions

**London Districts:** The City (financial/corp), Palace/Estates (government/aristocracy), West End (commerce/culture), Docklands (industrial), East End (diverse/underserved), The Village (bohemian/magical community)

## Formatting

All `.md` files are written for **Obsidian**. Use Obsidian-compatible markdown:
- Internal links with `[[double brackets]]`
- Tags with `#tag` syntax where appropriate
- Avoid HTML — use native markdown only
- Callouts use Obsidian syntax: `> [!note]`, `> [!warning]`, etc.

## Tone & Style Notes

**Primary references:** Fringe, X-Files, Ghostbusters — with a dash of Kingsman and Men in Black

- Paranormal investigation and artifact containment over traditional shadowrunner violence
- Institutional, professional operators — not rogue mercenaries
- Cases are weird, personal, and often morally ambiguous
- Magic is pervasive and institutionalised — not fringe
- London is heavily surveilled (Knight Errant private security, corporate oversight)
- Sophistication and discretion matter; the party's public reputation is an asset
