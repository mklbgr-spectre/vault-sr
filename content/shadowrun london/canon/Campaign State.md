## Adventure History

### Arc 1 — The Harrowgate Incident
- **Status:** Resolved
- **What we know:** The party was sent to Harrowgate Hall (a private magical academy outside London) to investigate paranormal disturbances caused by a failed student spirit summoning.
- **Cover:** Relay posed as a guest lecturer; others posed as Arcane Trials winners assisting with training.
- **Outcome:** The party defeated an amok-running phantom spirit and successfully prevented further harm to the students. No casualties.
- **Plot impact:** None — this was a self-contained mission with no threads leading into the larger campaign.
- **Significance:** This was the party's **swansong mission as Squires**. Their success led directly to promotion to Knight Errant.

### Arc 2 — The Metaplanar Journey
- **Status:** Resolved
- **Structure:** A Dr. Strange-esque journey through a series of metaplanes, each one a reflection of a different PC's inner world and backstory. Primarily a character showcase — the players got to reveal their characters' depths to each other.
- **Outcome:** All four PCs successfully **initiated** (advanced their magical abilities via the Shadowrun Anarchy initiation rules).
- **Plot impact:** None beyond character development — this was an inward journey, not an external mission.
- **Significance:** Major character moment. The planes visited were tied to each PC's metaplanar trial as described in their character sheets.

### Arc 3 — Smuggling in the Docks
- **Status:** Resolved (outcome details uncertain)
- **Handler:** Evelyn Drake (Knights of the Chalice — the party's superior)
- **Setup:** Detective Lucas Brennan (Lone Star) requested outside help — his face was too well-known in the Docklands. The Knights sent the party to assist, with orders to observe, gather evidence, and report back if magical artifacts were involved.
- **The Smuggling Ring:** A Vory v Zakone operation run by **Sergei "The Fisher" Ivanov** out of a warded Docklands warehouse, with **Viktor "Red" Morozov** as enforcer. They were moving a haul of black-market magical artifacts.
- **The Buyer:** **Nicholas "Nick the Shade" Dupont** — sharply dressed, faint French accent, buyer for an unknown client.
- **The Key Artifact:** Among the smuggled goods was the **Shard of Will** — an obsidian pendant with mental fortitude and illusion-piercing properties. Labeled *Artifact 1 of 5*, connected to an entity called **Xa'erithal Sutekh**. This is the first thread of what appears to be a larger campaign arc.
- **Part 2 (3.2):** Nick Dupont took the Shard of Will and moved it via a tightly choreographed handover operation in East End, using street samurai **Anton "Ironhand" Mikhailov** and rigger **Mark "Switch" Carter** as support.
- **Outcome:** The party cornered Dupont at the handover with Chimera Squad backup. The mysterious buyer was present. He smiled, and teleported away — impossible by Shadowrun magic rules. He bore a strong resemblance to the Lord Protector of London, though the party doesn't believe it was truly him. He seemed entirely unconcerned about losing the artifact. The **Shard of Will is now in Knights of the Chalice custody**.
- **Player takeaway:** There are a number of pre-Awakening artifacts out there. At least one person in London knows about them, is collecting them, and operates at a level that frightens everyone in the room.
- **Significance:** The mission that earned the party their promotion to Knight Errant. First brush with the campaign's main antagonist.

### Arc 4 — Ghost Train
- **Status:** Unknown — what actually happened?
- **Handler:** Sir Alistair Finch (Knights of the Chalice) — *note: S03 handler was Evelyn Drake, clarify if these are different superiors or the same person*
- **Setup:** Commuters began disappearing or experiencing severe memory distortion at Bow Road Station on the London Underground. The Knights were called in to investigate.
- **The Operation:** Two criminal gangs — **Pendragon Loop** (London) and **Spiegelgeest** (Rotterdam) — were exploiting a residual dimensional scar left by a classified WWII experiment called **Project Chimera**. Every night at 23:00, they used the anomaly to phase a single train car out of reality for 60 seconds, swap contraband through it, and phase it back. Witnesses experienced memory muddle and saw impossible blue-teal lights.
- **The Anomaly:** A "scar" in spacetime beneath the **Chapel of Shadows** — a centuries-old building sitting on a ley line nexus in East London. Project Chimera attempted localized spatial displacement using Tesla-era mad science in the 1940s. It worked, catastrophically, and left a permanent wound in reality. After the Awakening, the scar became magically active and exploitable.
- **Key antagonists:**
  - **Rex (Marcus Thorne)** — Technomancer ritualist, descendant of the original Project Chimera researcher. Obsessed with his ancestor's legacy. Gaunt, socially inept, brilliant. The mastermind.
  - **Bugbear (Kael Grom)** — Troll adept. Muscle. Has a Synch-Anchor cyberware implant letting him survive the dimensional shift, but it's slowly destroying his health. Secretly supports his younger sister Lana.
  - **Ellyth "Clicker" Vane** — Human tunnel runner and logistics. Wiry, agile, knows the Underground's hidden spaces intimately. Has a code of honor about protecting tunnel dwellers.
  - **Jurre van Daalen** — Rotterdam-side mage-engineer. Rex's intellectual counterpart.
- **Key witness:** **Brenda Higgins** — Ticket agent at Bow Road, initial tipster. Gruff, trustworthy, cynical about authority. Her son disappeared in a gang incident years ago. Potential recurring contact if treated well.
- **Missing person:** **Devon Karcz** — a regular commuter accidentally phased through to Rotterdam during a swap. His fate depends on player action.
- **The danger:** If the operation wasn't stopped quickly, a second incident would occur where someone gets "sheared" — caught on the threshold of the phase and partially unmade. A body partially in London, partially missing.
- **Meta-plot connection:** The mysterious buyer from Arc 3 secretly financed Rex — sending him start capital to rediscover Project Chimera, unearth the Chapel of Shadows site, and reconstruct the dimensional apparatus. Rex believed he was funding his own obsession. He was being used.
- **The second artifact:** Hidden within or integrated into Rex's machine was another of Xa'erithal Sutekh's artifacts. The players found it during their confrontation. *Which artifact is currently unknown — needs establishing.*
- **Outcome:** Adventure ran long and bled into the following session — approximately 2/3 resolved in one evening, the final confrontation and artifact discovery in the next.

### Arc 5 — The Oxford Express
- **Status:** Unknown — what actually happened?
- **Location:** Hermetic College of Oxford / Avalon Institute
- **Setup:** The Knights are called to investigate the death of **Professor Thaddeus Crowley**, Chair of Hermetic Ritual Magic — apparently killed during a graduation ceremony ritual. The official story is a magical malfunction.
- **The truth:** Crowley was a serial abuser who exploited, blackmailed, assaulted, and destroyed his students for decades. All 18 members of his graduating class conspired to sabotage the ritual, inverting its energy flow so it crystallised him from within. He is not dead — his body has become a sentient **astraline prism**, fully conscious and aware, trapped inside the statue.
- **The architect:** **Assistant Professor Marwen Vale** — himself a former Crowley student — recognised the students' plan and mathematically perfected it. He optimised the inversion so it would be undetectable individually but lethal collectively.
- **Crowley's actual fate:** Dead. The astraline prism angle was not used in play.
- **Key NPCs:**
  - **Victor Hawthorne** — student ringleader, distributed modified rune-strips to all 18
  - **Lydia Adebayo** — drafted the cover story; whispered the inverted formula during the ritual
  - **Owen Fraser** — Crowley's personal assistant; knows everything; the key witness
  - **Dr. Reginald Pembroke** — Headmaster; wants the Knights to blame Vale and close the case quietly
  - **Imani Okoro** — faculty secretary; can place Vale at the scene pre-ritual
  - **Dr. Beatrice Langley** — student counsellor who knew about the abuse and did nothing in time
  - **Artie Gleaves** — janitor; noticed ground humming and disturbed maintenance shafts before the ritual
- **Moral core:** Every perpetrator has a defensible motive. Crowley genuinely deserved it. There is no clean resolution.
- **No meta-plot connection:** Standalone. No link to the Patron or the artifacts.
- **Outcome:** The party chose not to pursue charges against the 18 students — too many powerful upper-class families involved, too much political blowback. The case was quietly dropped. Vale was offered a deal: conscripted into the Knights of the Chalice in exchange for the Order not pursuing his role in the murder. He is now a Knights asset, more or less against his will.

### Arc 7 — The Everett Copies
- **Status:** Resolved
- **Location:** Seattle, UCAS
- **Party:** Willow, Shane, Relay (Fariel absent — Relay's player Mike ran Solís as his table presence)
- **Handler:** Evelyn Drake
- **Setup:** KE Seattle flagged an unusual case via KE London to the Knights — five identical unidentified bodies found in the tree line north of Seattle, DNA corrupted, faces destroyed post-mortem. A British national, **Daniel Okafor**, kept appearing as the only lead but remained clean. Drake sent three Knights as outside eyes, official hook being consular interest in a British national.
- **The Truth:** Dr. Casimir Veth — contracted forensic pathologist for KE Seattle, and the party's own briefing consultant — had been running an unsanctioned magical cloning project for eight months from a rented lab in Everett. He was harvesting Okafor's aura during sleep from his flat directly across the street, using the imprint to produce copies. The copies were almost people — aware, frightened, and short-lived. He terminated and disposed of each one when it degraded. He processed his own work as the MCU's forensic consultant throughout.
- **The Flesh Artifact:** Worked into Veth's apparatus, acquired through a private collector, described by Veth as a powerful pre-Awakening focus. He never understood what it was. It was the reason his process worked at all — without it his theory would have remained a theory. The Patron had funded Veth specifically to keep it in active use, priming it. Veth believed he had a patient corporate backer. His Month 6 log entry shows he almost figured this out and chose not to pursue it.
- **Key NPC — Detective Yara Solís:** MCU detective and party contact throughout. Face adept. Had been running the case for six weeks, in regular contact with Veth, and her instincts returned nothing — because he never lied to her. Was present at the lab confrontation. Officially released the artifact into Knights custody at the party's request.
- **Improvised moment:** The party discussed going to Veth's apartment while standing in his earshot at the morgue. Veth sent a Spirit of Man to defend the apartment in anticipation. The party encountered it astrally and had to regroup to handle it together before proceeding.
- **Resolution:** Veth was talked down rather than fought. Currently in KE Seattle custody. The Knights have access for debrief. The Johnson thread (codename MERIDIAN-7, shell company Cascadia Biomedical Research LLC) was logged for the Knights' hacker division to pull later.
- **Artifact recovered:** The **Flesh fragment** — described by players as "a red ruby from Zelda with a beating heart glow inside." Now in Knights HQ vault. Fourth artifact in custody.
- **Meta-plot significance:** Before the briefing, the party used the Knights' library to research the artifacts they already hold. With their rolls, they learned: there are **6 artifacts total**, their fragment names (Mind, Will, Time, Threshold, Flesh, Power), and the sorcerer's name — **Xa'Suthek** — as a figure from Fourth World legend. This is now player-facing knowledge.

### Arc 6 — New Year's Eve at Highdark Hall
- **Status:** Most recent session — what actually happened?
- **Handler:** Commander Alistair Grey (Senior Knight Operations Coordinator — *possibly the same person as "Sir Alistair Finch" from S04, name inconsistency to resolve*)
- **Setup:** The party attends a New Year's Eve gala at **Highdark Hall**, a Georgian mansion in Mayfair, ostensibly as a low-stakes undercover observation job. Real threat: the host's girlfriend is a Black Veil operative executing a blood magic ritual at midnight.
- **The Antagonist:** **Vivienne Ashcroft** (real name: Vivienne Margaux Corbeau) — blood magic specialist and agent of the Black Veil. Posing as the girlfriend of widowed nobleman Lord Edmund Ashford. Known as "The Widow Maker" — three previous marks died after artifact acquisition. Previous operations: Prague (2080), Vienna (2081), Edinburgh (2082).
- **The Victim:** **Lord Edmund Ashford** — a grieving widower, magically naive, genuinely kind. Being manipulated via enchantment into hosting the ritual unknowingly. His late wife Charlotte was a senior Knight.
- **Lady Charlotte Ashford (deceased):** A Knights specialist in pre-Awakening magical archaeology. Died 3 years ago at Highdark Hall performing a *sacrificial sealing ritual* — she bound one of Xa'erithal Sutekh's artifacts into a temporal lock anchored to her own life force and the mansion's ley line convergence. Died to keep it out of the Patron's hands.
- **The Artifact:** The **Chronos Sigil** — allows the wielder to perceive and manipulate probability streams; essentially perfect precognition. Sealed in a "frozen moment" at the instant of Charlotte's death. Only accessible at a moment of temporal flux (midnight, New Year's).
- **The Ritual (Vivienne's plan):** A "Circle of Prosperity" — a fake New Year's tradition presented as charming and communal. At midnight, every magical guest in the circle becomes a node in a massive blood draining spell, their life force used as a battering ram to shatter Charlotte's seal. Most guests would survive but collapse. The artifact would be freed.
- **Key NPCs at the party:**
  - **Mrs. Helena Winters** (housekeeper, 35 years) — suspicious of Vivienne, Charlotte's confidante, key witness
  - **India Ravencroft** — Charlotte's goddaughter, hedge witch/diviner; Vivienne avoids her
  - **Dr. Margaret Ashworth** — Charlotte's former doctoral supervisor; notices the floor pattern is wrong
  - **Father Thomas Brennan** — priest/exorcist, Charlotte's friend; senses the house has been "wounded"
  - **Marcus Ashford** — Edmund's brother, no magic, correctly suspicious of Vivienne
  - **Alexei Volkov** — shady art dealer; red herring, unrelated to the plot
- **Meta-plot connection:** The Chronos Sigil is one of the five artifacts. Charlotte's sealed message may contain clues to the next artifact's location and warnings about the Patron.
- **Outcome:** The players stopped Vivienne before the ritual could harm anyone — by setting Highdark Hall on fire, disrupting the ritual entirely. No guests were hurt. Vivienne was taken into Knights custody. The Chronos Sigil's seal remains intact inside the mansion (or what's left of it). The Black Veil lost their operative and failed to retrieve the artifact.
