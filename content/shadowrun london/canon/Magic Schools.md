# Magic Schools of London

The UK's magical institutions are among the most prestigious in the Sixth World. London's schools dominate the annual [[Arcane Trials]] and produce the bulk of the country's corporate mages, government advisors, and independent practitioners.

---

## Hermetic College of Oxford

**Tradition:** Hermetic — formal arcane sciences
**Location:** Oxford

The most prestigious magical academy in Britain, and arguably the world. The College focuses on structured, academically rigorous magic rooted in ancient laws and formulae. Entry is highly competitive and strongly tied to existing connections with the nobility and corporate elite.

**Influence:** Graduates routinely enter high government, military, and corporate positions. Many of London's most powerful mages trained here. The College maintains close ties with aristocratic families and serves as the primary pipeline for arcane advisors to the Crown.

**Specialisations:** Advanced ritual magic, arcane law, alchemy, metamagic theory, enchantment, ley line research, and pre-Awakening artifact study.

**Character:** Conservative, hierarchical, deeply invested in tradition. The College views magic as a discipline demanding mastery of immutable laws — not creativity, not intuition. It produces brilliant technicians and reliable institutional operators, rarely revolutionaries.

---

## Avalon Institute

**Tradition:** Druidic — nature magic and spiritual arts
**Location:** Glastonbury (outside London)

Rooted in Celtic and druidic tradition, the Avalon Institute sits in the mystical countryside near Glastonbury, one of the most powerful ley line convergences in Britain. Students are trained to work in harmony with the land, nature spirits, and ancient heidnisch (pagan) gods rather than imposing structure upon them.

**Influence:** Less visible than Oxford in corporate circles, but deeply embedded in magical society. Many graduates become powerful druidic advisors, protectors of British magical sites, or quiet operatives working against corporate exploitation of the natural world. Some work alongside the [[Knights of the Chalice]] to preserve sacred sites.

**Specialisations:** Nature mastery, elemental magic, geomancy, shapeshifting, spiritualism, and communion with land spirits.

**Character:** Informal, communal, deeply suspicious of megacorporate interests. Where Oxford produces servants of institutions, Avalon produces guardians of something older. [[Willow]] studied here.

---

## Thames Academy for Thaumaturgy

**Tradition:** Corporate — technomagic and applied thaumaturgy
**Location:** London (near the corporate centre)

A modern school bankrolled by megacorporations — primarily NeoNET and Horizon — the Thames Academy exists to produce magical specialists for the corporate world. It aggressively bridges the gap between magic and technology, training mages who are as comfortable in a boardroom or a server room as they are on the astral plane.

**Influence:** Direct pipeline into megacorporate security, R&D, and espionage divisions. Many graduates work as corporate mages, magical security consultants, or are quietly folded into corporate intelligence operations. Some end up in intercompany proxy conflicts, using magic as a corporate weapon.

**Specialisations:** Technomagic, astral engineering, corporate thaumaturgy, matrix-integration spellcasting, magical security systems, and magical economics.

**Character:** Slick, ambitious, politically savvy. The Academy doesn't pretend magic is anything other than a tool for competitive advantage. Ethics are taught as risk management.

---

## Westminster Academy

**Tradition:** Enchantment and illusion
**Location:** London (Westminster)

Westminster trains the manipulators — the subtle operators who move through high society, political circles, and boardrooms using magic that leaves no visible trace. Its graduates are sought by noble families, political offices, and anyone who needs influence applied with a light touch.

**Influence:** Produces enchanters and illusionists who become advisors to the aristocracy, political fixers, covert operatives, and social engineers. Westminster alumni are disproportionately represented in London's most sensitive positions of power — the ones nobody talks about.

**Specialisations:** Charm magic, advanced illusion, subtle manipulation, enchantment, and political thaumaturgy.

**Character:** Elegant, discreet, and ruthless beneath the surface. Westminster students learn that the most effective magic is the kind nobody ever suspects was cast.

---

## Harrowgate Hall

**Type:** Pre-university preparatory academy
**Location:** Outside London (commuter distance)

A small but well-regarded magical academy for gifted students who aren't yet of university age. Catering to middle-income families — unlike the elite boarding schools that feed directly into Oxford — Harrowgate represents a meritocratic alternative for students who show early magical promise.

Hosts approximately 200–300 students with a faculty of experienced magicians. Notable as the location of the party's first mission as Squires — see [[Campaign State]].

## The Arcane Trials

An annual, nationally televised magical competition where each school submits a single student representative. Teams of four are randomly assembled from different schools and face labyrinthine arenas of magical traps, puzzle rooms, and drone-replicated creatures — each challenge uniquely designed for each group.

**Key facts:**
- Teams have 30 minutes per labyrinth; the event runs 2–3 days
- Judged on creativity, teamwork, and problem-solving — not just speed
- Heavily sponsored by AAA megacorporations; a major recruitment showcase
- Broadcast nationally and internationally; a cornerstone of UK magical culture
- One student per school; no direct individual competition, only team performance

**The Aztec Incident:** One year prior to the campaign's start, an Aztechnology-sponsored student abandoned their team mid-trial, sabotaged their teammates, and completed the labyrinth solo. Despite technically winning, the student was disqualified — the first in the Trials' history. Aztechnology initially defended the student, which only deepened the backlash. They were subsequently banned from sponsoring future Trials. Saeder-Krupp moved immediately to fill the vacuum, deepening the rivalry between the two corps. New rules preventing similar conduct were introduced; the incident remains a cautionary reference in UK magical circles.
