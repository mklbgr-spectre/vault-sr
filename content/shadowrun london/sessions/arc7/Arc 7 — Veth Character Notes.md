## At the Table — Actual Play Notes

**Cause of death — the copies:** Terminated by Veth using a cocktail of syringes. Clean, fast, medical. The toxicology pointed clearly to someone with pharmaceutical knowledge and clinical precision — a detail that was present in the forensic record the entire time the party was sitting across from him at the morgue. Nobody made the connection until later.

**On whether the copies were murder victims:** When the party pressed this at the confrontation, Veth's answer was: *"They were real humans for a few hours only. If I hadn't ended them when I did, they would have died a far worse death."*

He believed this. It's not entirely wrong. He created the conditions that made it true, decided termination was mercy, and used that mercy as moral cover for everything that preceded it. A closed loop he constructed carefully and has never examined from the outside.

This will be the central question in any formal debrief — and his answer will be the most revealing thing he says.

## At a Glance

Human male, early 50s. Eastern European origin. Forensic pathologist, contracted specialist for Knight Errant Seattle. Awakened — hermetic tradition, self-taught in biological imprint theory. Thin, precise, unhurried. Has the manner of someone who stopped caring about social performance a long time ago.

## Public Record — CV

What comes up on a Matrix search or KE personnel file:

**Born:** Kraków, Poland, 2034. Awakened at 19.

**Education:** MD, Jagiellowski Universität Kraków. Postgraduate specialisation in forensic thaumaturgy, Heidelberger Institut für Magische Wissenschaften, Germany. Graduated with distinction.
 
**Academic career:** Research fellow, Heidelberger Institut, 2058–2064. Published eight papers on forensic astral analysis and aura-based identification techniques, well-received in specialist circles. A ninth paper — *"Mnemonic Persistence in Astral Imprints: Toward a Theoretical Framework for Consciousness Reconstruction"* — submitted 2064, rejected by three peer reviewers as theoretically unsound. Fellowship not renewed. No further academic affiliation.

**Post-academic:** Contracted forensic work across several European jurisdictions, 2065–2073. Prague, Vienna, Warsaw. Competent and in-demand, no disciplinary record. Keeps to himself.

**Seattle:** Arrived 2083. Contracted to Knight Errant Seattle morgue within two months of arrival — straightforward hire, strong references. MCU consultant designation added four months later at Solís's recommendation. No flags.

> [!note] What the CV Doesn't Show
> The gap between the rejected paper and Seattle is eight years of someone quietly not giving up. Nothing in the public record explains why he moved to Seattle specifically. Nothing explains the shell company. To anyone doing a surface search, he's a private, competent specialist who took a morgue job far from home. Unremarkable.

## Shadow Amps

**Astral Projection** *(standard)*
Projects astrally. His default working state for harvesting — he's spent more time projected than embodied over the past eight months. Unusually comfortable and precise while projecting.

**Aura Dissection** *(custom — Amp Level 2)*
Veth's astral combat method. Rather than brute force, he interferes with a target's aura at a structural level — precise, quiet, and deeply unpleasant. Targets Willpower rather than physical defense. On a hit, deals Stun damage and imposes a –1 die penalty to all magical actions until the end of the scene as the target's aura tries to compensate.

**Imprint Harvest** *(custom — Amp Level 3, situational)*
His core research technique. Requires the target to be asleep, unconscious, or fully unaware. Pulls a deep imprint of the target's aura and surface memory structure over ~90 minutes. Not usable in combat. Relevant if the party tries to understand what he did to Okafor — Relay or Willow examining Okafor's aura will recognise the signature of this technique.

## Scene Notes

Rolls Medicine + Logic (8 dice) against nothing — he's not being opposed, he's presenting. Use this pool if a player tries to catch him in a factual error or pushes on a specific claim. He will win that exchange. He processed the bodies himself.

**Scene 4 — Confrontation:**
Opens with talking, not combat. Gun comes out when he accepts it's ending. Targets Shane with the gun (physical deterrent), Willow astrally (unknown tradition, higher magical threat to him than Relay). Uses Aura Dissection — 8 dice astral combat — precise and deliberate rather than aggressive. No contingency plan. He is improvising from the moment they walk in.
