# London

London in the Sixth World is a city of stark contrasts — steeped in centuries of history yet brimming with cutting-edge technology. Corporate power, ancient tradition, and magical phenomena collide daily. It is one of the most magically dense cities in the world and one of the most heavily surveilled.

---

## Government & Law

The city is technically governed by the British Parliament, but the **Lord Protector** holds significant practical power — acting as a bridge between the Crown, megacorporations, and the various factions competing for influence. The role is political, diplomatic, and deliberately opaque.

Law enforcement is handled almost entirely by private corporate security, primarily **Knight Errant**. What remains of the **Metropolitan Police Service (MPS)** handles only limited public functions. In practice, who polices you depends entirely on whose territory you're standing in.

Surveillance is pervasive: drones, cameras, and Matrix monitoring blanket the city in the interest of corporate security. High-end districts add astral warding and magical monitoring on top — unauthorised rituals in the wrong postcode are detected within minutes.

---

## Megacorp Presence

Megacorporations dominate London — controlling vast real estate, maintaining private armies, and operating extraterritorial enclaves where local law does not apply.

- **Saeder-Krupp** — the dominant player. Operates the massive South London Arcology and has fingers in government, academia, and magical research.
- **Horizon** — controls significant Matrix infrastructure and media.
- **NeoNET** — major presence in technomagic and corporate communications.
- **Aztechnology** — reputation severely damaged in London following the [[Magic Schools#The Aztec Incident|Aztec Incident]]. Influence sharply reduced.

---

## Society & Culture

London is deeply stratified. The ultra-rich live in opulence behind magical wards and private security; the lower classes survive in dense, polluted sprawl with minimal corporate protection. Despite its technological advancement, the city holds fiercely to old traditions — knighthood, aristocracy, and ceremonial magic remain genuinely influential.

**Metatypes:**
- Humans dominate corporate and political circles
- Orks and trolls form the backbone of industrial and security labour, and face significant discrimination
- Elves are associated with magical academia and high society
- Dwarves play prominent roles in engineering and technomancy
- Satyrs work as performers, athletes, and thaumaturgical specialists
- Pixies appear occasionally in magical and environmental circles

## Districts

### The City & The Temple
The financial heart of London — skyscrapers, corporate headquarters, and the constant hum of high-value transactions. **The Temple** is a subsection used by lawyers, banks, and arcane consultants, protected by magical wards and corporate mages. **Temple Church** serves as a site of magical arbitration between factions who need neutral ground.

Notable: Stock Exchange Tower, Lloyd's Arcology.

### The Palace & The Estates
Buckingham Palace and the surrounding aristocratic estates. Home to London's political elite and the machinery of the Crown. Heavily secured — magical defences and private security are constant. Corporate influence here operates through social access rather than direct presence.

Notable: Buckingham Palace, The Royal Vault.

### West End & Westway
Entertainment, luxury retail, and high-end commercial districts. The West End is famous for its theatres; Westway is exclusive corporate housing. Magical activity here is subtle — enchantment and social manipulation, not visible displays. High-profile negotiations and political deals are a constant undercurrent.

### The Docklands & The Dogs
Once a thriving shipping hub, now a patchwork of corporate warehouses, industrial zones, and gentrified tech enclaves. The older, derelict sections are haunted by awakened creatures and rogue spirits. Criminal operations — including artifact smuggling — run through the Docklands regularly. The Vory v Zakone maintain significant presence here.

Notable: The Cradle (criminal hub), various corporate shipping facilities.

### East End
Run-down, diverse, and largely underserved by corporate infrastructure. Known for black-market bazaars, underground fight clubs, illegal talismonger activity, and a strong sense of community that mistrusts outsiders.

Notable: Little Egypt, London Stadium (repurposed for illegal blood sports and rumoured supernatural activity).

#### Little Egypt

A dense few streets in the East End where London's Egyptian diaspora has put down roots — immigrants, refugees, and the descendants of both, many of whom fled anti-magic crackdowns in Egypt and found the UK more tolerant of their traditions. It is not a glamorous neighbourhood. The streets are narrow, the buildings worn, the air thick with cumin, incense, and the faint metallic undertone of active ritual work. Corporate infrastructure is minimal. Community infrastructure is not.

The area operates on its own terms. Outsiders are not unwelcome, but they are noticed. Practitioners of other traditions are received with polite wariness until they prove themselves respectful of what the community has built here. The Egyptian tradition has a long memory and a strong sense of what belongs to it.

**The Hall of Ma'at** anchors the neighbourhood — a converted Victorian warehouse that serves simultaneously as community hall, temple, training space, and magical court. Ma'at is the principle of truth, cosmic order, and righteous balance, and the Hall takes that mandate seriously. Disputes within the community are brought here for arbitration before they go anywhere near corporate security or civil law. The building's wards are old and deep — layered by generations of practitioners, each adding to what came before. Assensing the Hall feels like reading a document written by a hundred different hands.

The Hall hosts:
- **Weekly community gatherings** — social, cultural, not exclusively magical
- **Instruction in Egyptian magical tradition** — open to practitioners of other traditions by invitation, not as a public offering
- **Mediation and arbitration** — the Hall's senior practitioners serve as judges under Ma'at's principle; their rulings carry real weight within the community
- **Ritual space** — available to practitioners in good standing; the convergence of decades of accumulated working makes it unusually potent

Beyond the Hall, the streets offer what the rest of the East End offers, plus what the community has added:
- Talismongers who stock components specific to Egyptian ritual practice — papyrus, natron, particular resins — sourced through networks that don't advertise
- A handful of tea houses and food stalls that are social hubs as much as businesses
- Practitioners who take private clients — readings, cleansings, protective work — with varying degrees of legal standing

**Relay (Nadia Safiya)** is known here. The community is not large enough for someone of her tradition and her abilities to be anonymous, and her work with the Knights gives her a complicated reputation — respected, and regarded with the mild suspicion that institutional affiliation always earns in a neighbourhood that has learned not to trust institutions.

### The Village
A unique district known for eco-friendly architecture, experimental design, and a bohemian magical community. Research facilities test the boundaries of both magic and technomancy here. Home to the Village branch of **Mortimer of London**.

Notable: [[Mortimer of London]] — a high-end tailor shop concealing the secret entrance to the Knights' underground bullet train network.

### Saeder-Krupp Arcology *(South London)*
A massive, self-contained corporate complex housing thousands of employees. Luxury and extreme security in equal measure. Effectively a city within a city — extraterritorial, governed by Saeder-Krupp law.

### Lambeth Containment Zone *(South London)*

Known to its residents as **The Squeeze**. Cordoned off by the British Army following a series of riots in 2038 that the government has never fully acknowledged as its own fault.

**What actually happened — Project 42-20:** For fifteen years, Adams-Hoffman corporation secretly experimented on the Lambeth population with experimental mutagens, ostensibly attempting to control or halt goblinization. The results were catastrophic: premature births, severe birth defects, immune system collapse, organ failure. Confirmed deaths: 750. Suspected deaths attributable to the project: 9,500. Hundreds more are still slowly dying from long-term mutagen exposure. In 2038, Ork journalists from Channel 7 broke the story in a single broadcast — which was never aired again.

The riots started fifteen days later. They were violently suppressed. From the survivors, the **Lambeth Martyrs** formed — an armed resistance organisation funded initially by criminal gangs, radicalised by grief. Their attacks killed nearly 100 people in one incident and 74 in another, including a former Prime Minister. The Army sealed the district. It has not been unsealed since.

**The Squeeze — historical context (2038–2050s):** In the immediate aftermath, the government called the residents rebels and the residents called the Army an occupation force. Every major road was blocked by Army checkpoints; residents were required to carry ID at all times; the Lambeth Martyrs kept recruiting from a population with nothing left to lose. Murals covered every surface. All gangs answered to the LM. Those who crossed them ended up at **the Metal Jungle** — the district's dump.

**Current state (2080s):** Forty-plus years have passed since the original cordon. What the LCZ looks like now — whether the Army presence has evolved, been replaced, or simply calcified; whether the Lambeth Martyrs still operate or have fragmented; whether Adams-Hoffman faced any meaningful accountability — is undefined and to be established when relevant.

> [!note] GM Note
> The 1st edition lore establishes the origin and the shape of the wound. What 45 years of Sixth World history did to that wound is yours to decide. The moral core remains: this was a corporate atrocity, the government enabled it, and the people living there are still paying for it. Whatever the current surface looks like, that foundation doesn't change.
>
> The magical dimension is worth keeping regardless of current politics — sustained collective trauma in a magically active city leaves astral marks. Practitioners working in the LCZ should feel that history pressing on them.)

## Magic in London

London is one of the most magically active cities in the Sixth World. Its history, ley lines, and deep occult traditions make it uniquely powerful — and uniquely dangerous.

**Ley Line Nexus:** A powerful convergence of ley lines runs beneath the city, particularly concentrated in The City and around ancient sites such as St. Paul's Cathedral. Magical activity near these nodes is amplified significantly.

**The Thames:** The river's mana flow is unstable. Some stretches are highly magical; others are dangerously polluted with magical residue from centuries of ritual activity and more recent corporate dumping.

**Glastonbury** *(outside London):* A centre for druidic magic deeply tied to ancient Celtic traditions. Home to the [[Magic Schools#Avalon Institute|Avalon Institute]].

**Egyptian tradition** is prominent in London, as many practitioners fled anti-magic policies in Egypt and found refuge in the UK. Little Egypt in the East End is the community's heart.

## Criminal & Paranormal Threats

**Criminal organisations:**
- **Vory v Zakone** — strong Docklands presence, artifact smuggling
- **Triads, Mafia, Vory, and smaller gangs** — varying involvement in magical crime and artifact trafficking
- Lambeth Containment Zone is rife with gang activity

**Paranormal threats:**
- **Shadow spirits** — rumoured to roam the Thames and Lambeth
- **Shedim** — occasionally reported in abandoned areas
- **Awakened creatures** — rogue basilisks in the Docklands, leyline anomalies across the city

## The Knights of the Chalice

The [[Knights of the Chalice]] are primarily a British organisation, but their mandate extends internationally when British magical interests are at risk — stolen artifacts being moved abroad, foreign cults using UK-origin relics, or direct threats to the nation's magical infrastructure. Outside the UK they operate undercover, avoid direct confrontation with local authorities, and work through shadow networks rather than official channels.
