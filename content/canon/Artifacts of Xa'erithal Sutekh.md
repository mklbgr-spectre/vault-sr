act 4 thingy, i remember it describing as a # Artifacts of Xa'erithal Sutekh

In the Fourth World, the immortal sorcerer Xa'erithal Sutekh was too powerful to destroy. The Seven Banishers shattered his essence into **six artifacts** and scattered them across the world. Each contains a different fragment of his being. Together, they can restore him.

The artifacts survived into the Sixth World largely dormant — mistaken for myth, relics, or mundane objects. As mana levels rise, they are reawakening.

> [!warning] GM Eyes Only
> Players know only that pre-Awakening artifacts exist and that someone is collecting them. They do not know the number, the fragments, or Xa'erithal Sutekh's name.

## The Shard of Will

**Fragment:** Will
**Form:** A smooth obsidian pendant, roughly the size of a large coin, shaped like a fractured teardrop. Silver inlays form an ever-shifting arcane sigil, visible only under certain lighting. Cool to the touch even in high heat. Emits a faint humming vibration near strong ley lines or when attuned to a user.

**Abilities:**
- **Mental Fortitude** *(passive)* — the wearer becomes increasingly resistant to mind-affecting effects, social manipulation, intimidation, and magical coercion
- **Unwavering Sight** *(activated)* — for a time, the wearer sees through illusions and magical glamours. Does not grant astral sight; only pierces false images

**Drawbacks:**
- Worn for extended periods without rest: intrusive thoughts, flashes of forgotten knowledge, strong déjà vu
- Prolonged use subtly hardens the wearer's will — resistance to manipulation grows, but so does stubbornness and inflexibility
- Runes flicker and pulse faintly when near another Xa'erithal artifact

**History:** Recovered from a Vory v Zakone smuggling operation in the Docklands. Was being sold to Nick Dupont on behalf of the Patron. The Patron appeared at the handover, smiled, and teleported away — unbothered by losing it.

## The Liminal

**Fragment:** Threshold
**Form:** A small floating cube of impossible geometry — resembling a tesseract, but wrong in ways that are hard to articulate. Deep blues and purples shift across its surface like light through a prism that doesn't obey normal physics. It does not rest on surfaces; it hovers at roughly chest height and slowly rotates when unattended. Looking at it too long creates a faint vertigo, as though the room is slightly less solid than it was a moment ago.

**Abilities:** Connected to dimensional shifting — the crossing of boundaries between realities. Was integrated into Rex's apparatus at the Chapel of Shadows as the core component enabling the planar swap. Its standalone abilities outside the machine are undefined — whether it can be wielded directly, or only through constructed apparatus, is unknown.

**History:** Hidden inside the dimensional apparatus built by Rex (Marcus Thorne) beneath the Chapel of Shadows in East London. Rex had been unknowingly funded by the Patron to rediscover the Project Chimera site and reconstruct the experiment — the artifact was the real prize all along. Recovered by the party during the confrontation (Arc 4).

## The Chronos Sigil

**Fragment:** Time
**Form:** A thin disc of polished silver, palm-sized, its surface etched with concentric rings of microscopic glyphs that shift almost imperceptibly when not being directly observed. Held up to light, the disc appears to show faint overlapping reflections — not of the room around it, but of somewhere else, or somewhen else. It is always slightly warm, as though it has been resting in a hand just a moment before.

**Abilities:**
- Allows the wielder to perceive and manipulate probability streams — essentially perfect, actionable precognition. Far beyond what normal divination magic can achieve. Where other seers see suggestions, the Chronos Sigil shows *paths*.

**History:** Sealed by **Lady Charlotte Ashford** (senior Knight, deceased) inside Highdark Hall, Mayfair. Charlotte sacrificed herself to lock it into a temporal seal anchored to her life force and the mansion's ley line convergence. The Black Veil operative Vivienne Ashcroft attempted to break the seal at a New Year's Eve gala — foiled by the party. The artifact was extracted by Knights experts during party downtime (Arc 6).

## The Patron's Artifact (The Mind Fragment)

**Fragment:** Mind
**Form:** Unknown
**Abilities:** Unknown — but the Mind fragment never went dormant. It has been subtly influencing an individual across centuries, shaping the Black Veil from within. Whether the Patron *carries* it, *is* it, or has been *shaped by* it over lifetimes is the central mystery.

## The Flesh Artifact *(unnamed)*

**Fragment:** Flesh
**Form:** Unknown
**Location:** Unknown
**Abilities:** Unknown — but the Flesh fragment suggests control or manipulation of the physical body. Body horror direction: reanimation, corpse puppeteering, forced transformation, or something that blurs the line between living and dead.

> [!note] Adventure Hook
> This one screams body horror — zombies, grotesque physical transformation, something viscerally wrong. The artifact likely surfaces in a context where bodies are already involved: a hospital, a morgue, a cult, a black-market biotech operation.

## The Power Artifact *(unnamed)*

**Fragment:** Power
**Form:** Unknown
**Location:** Unknown
**Abilities:** Unknown — but the Power fragment implies raw magical amplification. Two directions being considered:
> - **Magical amplification** — the bearer becomes a terrifyingly overpowered mage. Effectively unlimited magical output; the "no spell slot" nightmare scenario.
> - **Perfect telekinesis** — absolute physical mastery over matter at range. Subtle in the wrong hands, catastrophic at scale.

> [!note] Adventure Hook
> Either version is most frightening in the hands of someone who already knows how to use power — a corporate mage, a political figure, a desperate person with nothing to lose. The artifact probably doesn't announce itself loudly; it just makes whoever holds it *quietly unstoppable* until it's too late.

## The Broken Eye

The Black Veil's symbol. A stylised eye — iris intact, outer structure shattered into six pieces, each representing one artifact. Found hidden in corporate seals, arcane sigils, and digital imprints. Always in plain sight. Never acknowledged.
