# Knights of the Chalice

A secretive British order dedicated to securing magical artifacts, neutralising paranormal threats, and maintaining the integrity of the UK's magical landscape. Where conventional law enforcement stops at the edge of the supernatural, the Knights begin.

The party are active members — currently [[Campaign State#Party Status|Knight Errant rank]].

---

## Role & Mandate

The Knights function as a mix of magical MI7 and supernatural investigators. They are not shadowrunners — discretion, reputation, and institutional trust matter. Reckless actions draw unwanted attention; careful work builds influence within the Order and beyond.

**Core operations:**
- Artifact retrieval and containment
- Paranormal investigation and threat neutralisation
- Protection of British magical heritage and ley line infrastructure
- Arcane espionage countermeasures

**International reach:** Primarily a British organisation, but the mandate extends abroad when British interests are directly at risk — tracking stolen artifacts, intercepting foreign cults using UK-origin relics, or responding to threats that originate overseas. Outside the UK, Knights operate undercover, avoid confrontations with local authorities, and work from the shadows rather than through official channels.

---

## Structure & Ranks

The Order is merit-based. Promotion reflects demonstrated competence, judgement, and contribution to the Order's mission — not seniority.

| Rank | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Squire** | Entry rank. Probationary. Supervised operations. |
| **Knight Errant** | Full operative status. Current party rank. |
| **Knight** | Senior operative. Independent command authority. |
| **Knight Captain** | Field command. Oversees teams and larger operations. |
| **Knight Commander** | Strategic leadership. Runs divisions or regions. |
| **Knight Regent** | Apex rank. Governance of the Order itself. |

Members receive a salary and accommodations commensurate with rank. Gear, magical foci, and operational support can be requisitioned through the Order.

## Support & Resources

**Cyber Department:** The Order maintains an in-house team of deckers and matrix specialists. Operatives can request digital support — unlocking doors, disabling cameras, intelligence gathering — as needed per mission. Availability is limited by time and legal risk; it is not an unlimited resource.

**Requisitions:** Gear, magical foci, and specialist equipment can be drawn from Order stores. What's available depends on rank and operational context.

## Headquarters

### Mortimer of London — The Village Branch

Mortimer of London is a worldwide tailor chain with a well-earned reputation: their suits are impeccably cut, hold up under serious wear, and carry discreet ballistic underlayers that make them equally suitable for a board meeting and a firefight. The chain is a fixture of the Sixth World — shadowrunners, fixers, Johnsons, and executives alike know the name. It requires no explanation and draws no suspicion.

The Village branch is quieter than most. No walk-ins, appointments preferred. The shop floor is small and understated — dark wood panelling, a handful of bolts of exceptional fabric, two staff who speak only when spoken to and measure with uncanny precision. The suits in the window are real and genuinely available. Real clients come here. The shop is not a front; it simply has a second purpose its clients don't know about.

Towards the back, past the fitting rooms, there is a door marked *Alterations — Staff Only*. Knights operatives are given a simple authentication — a physical token, a code word, a biometric, or some combination, updated periodically — that opens it. Beyond is a descending staircase, and below that, a private platform.

### The Platform & The Network

The platform is clean, well-lit, and silent in the way of places that are rarely crowded. It carries no signage beyond a single stylised chalice embossed on the wall at the far end. The train that arrives is compact — a few carriages, fast, smooth, completely off the public network. It connects Knights facilities across the country: the main HQ, regional outposts, a handful of secure storage sites.

Travel time to the main HQ is short enough that operatives treat it as a commute. The carriages are functional rather than comfortable — bench seating, good lighting, secure document storage built into the walls. Classified briefings sometimes happen on the train. There is no Matrix access onboard; conversations stay in the carriage.

> [!note] GM Note
> The Mortimer of London reference is intentional on two levels — players who know Shadowrun lore will recognise it as the armoured suit shop, and then clock that it's also the Kingsman tailor entrance. Both readings are correct. The shop genuinely sells its suits; Knights operatives are among its best customers.
