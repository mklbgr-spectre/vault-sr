# Homebrew — Psychometry (Anarchy)

> [!note] Design Note
> Psychometry has no equivalent in Shadowrun Anarchy. This homebrew adapts the SR5 rules (*Street Grimoire*, pp. 144–146) to fit Anarchy's lighter framework. The SR5 source material is preserved in full in [[Metamagic — Psychometry]].
>
> Acquisition prerequisites are not tracked in this campaign. Psychometry is treated as a Shadow Amp the character possesses.

---

## What It Is

Psychometry is the magical ability to read emotional and sensory impressions left on objects, places, and people by past events. Every significant experience leaves an echo on the astral plane — a psychometrist can deliberately access those echoes through touch.

The ability is pervasive and always present. Once learned, every object the magician touches generates some degree of feedback. She must constantly maintain mental filters to block the noise of ordinary life. Lowering those filters for a deliberate reading takes only moments — but there is no way to stop a reading once it begins.

---

## Shadow Amp Entry

**Psychometry**
*Amp Level 2 — Awakened only*

The character can read emotional and sensory impressions left on objects, places, and people by past events. To perform a deliberate reading, she lowers her mental shields and makes a **Psychometry Test** (see below). The reading cannot be stopped once begun. While the reading is ongoing, she suffers a **–2 modifier to all die rolls**.

---

## Performing a Reading

### Step 1 — Contact and Intent
The magician must be in physical contact with the object (or present at a location). She lowers her mental shields — a deliberate act taking only a few seconds, no roll required. Announce the attempt.

### Step 2 — Assemble the Dice Pool

**Base pool:** `Sorcery + Willpower`

Apply all relevant modifiers from the [[#Modifier Table]] below. Add bonus dice for favourable conditions; subtract dice for unfavourable ones. The minimum pool is 1 die.

### Step 3 — Roll Against Difficulty

The GM sets a difficulty pool based on how legible the impression is, using the table below as a baseline:

| Baseline Difficulty | Dice |
|---|---|
| Clear impression (within a day, strong emotion) | 6 dice |
| Average impression | 8 dice |
| Faded or degraded impression | 10 dice |
| Ancient or heavily obscured impression | 12 dice |

This difficulty pool is *before* applying the modifier table — modifiers are applied to the player's roll, not the GM's pool. The GM is free to adjust the base difficulty for unusual circumstances.

### Step 4 — Interpret Results

Count **net hits** (your hits minus GM hits):

| Net Hits | What the Magician Perceives |
|---|---|
| 0 or fewer | Nothing. The reading fails — static, silence, or a sense of absence. |
| 1 | Flashes. Disconnected emotions, a colour, a sound fragment, a vague sensation. Almost nothing usable. |
| 2 | Impressions. A mood, a general sense of what happened, partial and unstable images. |
| 3 | Detail. Multi-sensory sequences — sights, sounds, smells. The emotional context is clear. Specific individuals may be recognisable. |
| 4+ | Coherent visions. Lengthy, vivid sequences with significant identifying information. The magician experiences the impression almost as if present. |

> [!note] Tradition Flavour
> The reading is not a literal recording — it is interpreted through the magician's paradigm and symbolism. The *information content* at each tier is the same regardless of tradition, but the presentation differs. A hermetic mage might experience ordered symbolic constructs and geometric precision; a shaman might see spirit-visions and dream imagery; a practitioner of Egyptian traditions might receive the impression as a scroll of hieroglyphs or the presence of a particular deity's aspect.

### Step 5 — Duration
The reading **cannot be stopped early**. Once begun, it runs for a GM-set or GM-rolled duration:

- Roll 1D6: on a 1–2, the reading lasts **1 minute**; on a 3–4, **5 minutes**; on 5–6, **longer** (GM discretion, appropriate to the weight of the impression).
- The GM may simply decide the duration if it better serves the scene.

The magician suffers **–2 to all die rolls** for the entire duration.

## Modifier Table

Apply these modifiers as **additional or removed dice** from the Sorcery + Willpower base pool before rolling.

### Age of Impression

| Time Since Impression Was Made | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Within 1 day | +0 |
| Within 1 week | –2 |
| Within 1 month | –4 |
| Within 1 year | –6 |
| Within 1 decade | –8 |
| Each additional decade | –2 |

> [!note] Isolated Locations
> Objects sealed away from other auras — in tombs, sealed vaults, abandoned places — decay far more slowly. The GM may treat such an item as if it were much more recent (reduce the time modifier by one or two steps).

### Nature of Object or Impression

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Object has a strong personal connection to the event (wedding ring, murder weapon, beloved toy) | +2 |
| The magician had a personal connection to the person who left the impression | +2 |
| The impression involves intense emotion or violence | +3 |
| The impression involves a violent death | +4 |
| Item belongs to someone and is strongly associated with them (daily-worn, personally significant) | +2 |
| Object is very large — a building, a location over 500 square metres | –2 |
| Object carries multiple significant impressions | –1 per additional impression |
| The impression has been deliberately obscured (Smudging ritual, astral erasure) | –2 to –4 (GM discretion) |
| Each repeated attempt on the same object in the same session | –2 cumulative |

## Always-On Noise

Outside of deliberate readings, the magician's filters handle the constant low-level feedback of the world. This is a background fact of her existence, not a mechanical burden — but it is a roleplaying texture. She may occasionally receive unsolicited fragments from objects she handles in emotionally charged moments, at the GM's discretion. These are never mechanically harmful; they are flavour, and occasionally a GM-offered clue.

---

## Countermeasures

Sophisticated criminals, intelligence operatives, and high-level mages know psychometry exists.

- **Smudging:** A ritual cleansing that permanently degrades the astral imprint at a location or on an object. If a scene has been smudged before the magician arrives, apply a –2 to –4 penalty (GM sets based on how thorough the smudging was). The Knights' forensic teams can identify whether smudging has occurred.
- **Repeated handling by many people:** A busy, well-trafficked object accumulates layers of impressions (–1 per significant impression, stacking).
- **Time:** The most reliable countermeasure — old impressions are simply harder to read.
