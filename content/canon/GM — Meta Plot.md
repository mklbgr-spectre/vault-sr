# GM — Meta Plot: The Return of Xa'erithal Sutekh

> [!warning] GM Eyes Only
> Players know only that pre-Awakening artifacts are circulating in London and that someone in the city is aware of them. They know nothing else on this page.

---

## The Forgotten Sorcerer

In the **Fourth World** (the high-magic era of Earthdawn), an immortal sorcerer named **Xa'erithal Sutekh** sought to bend magic itself to his will. Too powerful to be destroyed, a coalition of legendary heroes, mages, and warriors — later mythologised as **The Seven Banishers** — shattered his essence into **six artifacts** and scattered them across the world.

These artifacts survived into the Fifth World largely dormant, mistaken for myth, relics, or mundane objects. Now, in the Sixth World, as mana levels rise, the pieces are reawakening — and an unknown force is moving to gather them.

---

## The Six Artifacts

*Full artifact details in [[Artifacts of Xa'erithal Sutekh]].*

Each artifact contains a different fragment of Xa'erithal's being:

| Fragment | Artifact | Status |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Will** | The Shard of Will *(obsidian pendant)* | In Knights custody — recovered Arc 3 |
| **Time** | The Chronos Sigil | In Knights custody — sealed at Highdark Hall, extracted in downtime after Arc 6 |
| **Threshold** | The Liminal | In Knights custody — recovered from Rex's dimensional machine, Arc 4 |
| **Mind** | Unknown form | Never dormant; shaping events for centuries. Likely carried by or embodied in the Patron. |
| **Flesh** | Unknown form | **Active — Veth's lab, Seattle.** Being unknowingly used by researcher Dr. Casimir Veth to power a magical cloning process. Arc 7. |
| **Power** | Unknown | Undiscovered. |

The artifact containing his **Mind** is the most dangerous — it has never truly been dormant and has shaped the Black Veil from within over centuries.

## The Antagonist — The Patron

**Known pattern:** He does not retrieve artifacts himself when he can avoid it. He identifies people already obsessed with relevant sites or relics, funds them quietly, lets them do the dangerous excavation work, then collects. Rex (Arc 4) was given anonymous start capital to rediscover Project Chimera — he thought he was financing his own obsession. He was being used as a tool. The artifact hidden in Rex's machine was the actual prize all along.

**The priming theory:** The Patron may not simply need the artifacts gathered — he may need them *used*. Each artifact put to active magical purpose appears to prime it in some way, perhaps reawakening the fragment of Xa'erithal's essence within. If this is correct, the Patron's strategy is elegant and patient: find obsessives, fund them, let them work the artifact into their practice, then either collect it or let the Knights do so. By the time any artifact reaches the Knights' vault, it may already have served his purpose.

This reframes every artifact encounter: the Shard of Will (used before recovery, Arc 3), the Liminal (powering Rex's machine, Arc 4), the Chronos Sigil (sealed but actively suppressing something at Highdark Hall, Arc 6), and now the Flesh artifact (used extensively by Veth for months, Arc 7). The Knights have been inadvertently completing his ritual preparation every time they've shut down an artifact situation.

> [!warning] GM Note — The Trap
> If the priming theory is correct, the Knights' vault may be exactly where he wants the artifacts. Three primed fragments, gathered in one location, under wards he may already know how to breach. The HQ attack becomes not a desperate raid but a planned collection.

**First direct sighting:** At the end of Arc 3, the party cornered Nick Dupont at the artifact handover with Chimera Squad backup. The buyer was present.

- **He smiled arrogantly and teleported away.** Did not panic. Did not fight. Simply left.
- **Teleportation magic does not exist in Shadowrun.** This should be impossible. The players know this and were visibly shaken by it.
- **He bore a strong resemblance to the Lord Protector of London.** The players don't believe it was actually him — but the resemblance was unmistakable. The party attempted to assense him; no roll pierced the disguise. Whether it was truly the Lord Protector or a magical mask is deliberately left open — but the takeaway the players correctly drew is that he must be an extraordinarily high initiation mage, one operating well beyond anything they've encountered.
- **He was completely unbothered that the Knights intercepted the artifact.** This is the most unsettling detail. He either doesn't need that specific one yet, has others already, or knows something about the Knights' situation that they don't.

> [!note] GM Foreshadowing
> A possible future: once enough artifacts are accounted for — whether gathered by the Patron or by the Knights — he moves against the Knights directly. The HQ itself gets attacked. The Knights inadvertently becoming a vault for his artifacts is a trap he may have anticipated.
>
> **Vivienne's role in the attack — two options (decide later):**
> - **The Sacrifice** — she is not rescued, she is *spent*. Her blood or life force used remotely as a magical key or distraction to breach the HQ's wards from the inside. She may not even know this is her fate.
> - **The Plant** — she always knew capture was a possibility. She has been playing the long game in the brig, a force multiplier waiting to activate when the attack begins.
>
> The ambiguity is the point. She should be played as a Khan-in-the-brig figure throughout — cryptic, controlled, dangerously charming, impossible to read. The party should never feel certain which it is.

## The Black Veil

A covert, highly influential organisation working to recover all six artifacts and resurrect Xa'erithal Sutekh.

- **Symbol:** The Broken Eye — a stylised eye with a whole iris but a shattered outer structure, cracked into six pieces. Found hidden in corporate seals, arcane sigils, and digital imprints. Always in plain sight.
- **Structure:** Lower members believe they are simply acquiring rare magical artifacts for a greater cause. Only the inner circle knows the true purpose.
- **Reach:** Embedded in governments, megacorporations, and magical institutions.
- **The Leader:** May already be partially or fully possessed by Xa'erithal Sutekh — or may already *be* him in some form.

---

## What the Knights of the Chalice Know

The Knights are aware of Xa'erithal Sutekh only as **The Tale of the Seven Banishers** — an old legend, largely dismissed as folklore. The details in their records are murky and treated as myth.

They know a shadowy organisation is behind the rising demand for pre-Awakening relics, but have **not yet connected it to Xa'erithal Sutekh**.

## Player-Facing Seeds Already Planted

- The Shard of Will encountered in Arc 3 — players know it is significant but not why
- At least one person in London (connected to the Black Veil) is aware of the artifacts and collecting them
- The Broken Eye symbol may appear on artifacts or in the margins of relevant locations

---

## Escalation Path

1. Players disrupt artifact trades, unknowingly unravelling the conspiracy piece by piece
2. Some artifacts may already be in corporate or magical research hands — competing factions emerge
3. As more pieces converge, Xa'erithal begins subtly manifesting — minor magical anomalies around his relics
4. **Final twist:** If all six artifacts are gathered, Xa'erithal may no longer need a physical form at all
