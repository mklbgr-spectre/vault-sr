💠 **Dreamer #1: Freya Halvorsen**

- **Age:** 18
- **Major:** Comparative Literature (Harvard College)
- **Court Relationship:** Reverie-based Dreamer (unenchanted)
- **Connection:** Protégé, mirror, gentle muse


**Narrative Summary:**  
Before her Chrysalis, Mira — though already quietly struggling herself — found Freya Halvorsen: a brilliant but fragile freshman whose passion for literature burned too hot and fast, already veering toward emotional exhaustion. Freya’s glacier-blue eyes, constant reading, and too-heavy tote bags marked her as someone who lived more in stories than in the present.

Mira recognized a mirror of her own younger self and took Freya under her wing in an informal, almost secretive mentorship: late-night talks, scribbled annotations slipped into books, encouragement without pressure. These small kindnesses stabilized Freya and quietly fostered her creativity.

After Mira’s return from Europe — now knowingly fae — she kept that connection intact, careful never to enchant or overwhelm Freya, allowing her to remain mortal while still touching the edge of wonder. Freya senses something different about Mira but can’t articulate it; instead, she channels those feelings into her writing: secret poems, dream fragments, visions of veiled mentors and night gardens.

For Mira, **Freya is more than just a Dreamer — she’s a living metaphor for the life Mira might have had if her Chrysalis hadn’t intervened. Protecting and nurturing Freya’s creative spark is both an emotional tether and an ongoing source of Glamour.**
