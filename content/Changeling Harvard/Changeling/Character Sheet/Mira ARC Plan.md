# Arc Planning: The Seelie Inquisitor

> [!warning] OOC / Meta Planning
> This document summarizes a planning discussion about Myrrha's long-term character arc. Not all of this is in play yet, and none of it has been formally discussed with the GM.

---

## The Endpoint Vision

The hypothetical fully-realized version of this arc: **a self-assured, warm, quietly devastating woman that other people warn you not to underestimate.**

The warmth is real. The care is genuine. The devastation is also real — and all three coexist without contradiction. She doesn't become cruel. She becomes *just*. Which can be far more frightening.

### Reference Characters

**Closest fits:**
- **Chrisjen Avasarala (The Expanse)** — the single closest reference. Genuine warmth and relationships, complete ruthlessness in service of what she believes is right, institutional authority that is also personally formidable. Unfailingly polite until she isn't, and the switch is surgical.
- **Minerva McGonagall** — warm teacher, genuine care, will destroy you with complete composure
- **Moiraine Damodred (Wheel of Time)** — serene, genuine, quietly terrifying institutional authority
- **Olenna Tyrell** — warm, devastating through social precision
- **Judi Dench's M** — institutional, maternal, immovable
- **Marge Gunderson (Fargo)** — warmth completely genuine, competence total, underestimated until it's too late

**Partial fits worth noting:**
- Poppy Adams (Kingsman 2) — the warmth/ruthlessness coexistence
- Alfred Bester (Babylon 5) — the Psi Corps institutional investigator energy
- Claire Underwood — fits mechanically but drifts too cold
- Nikita — useful as an *origin point* archetype, not an endpoint

### The Avasarala Path

Avasarala implies a history the show never shows: the composure reads like something earned through having been less composed. The precision reads like someone who once used blunter instruments and learned their limits.

**Weapon → Operator → Institution**

For Mira the arc runs in reverse entry order from Nikita — she already has the humanity, what she's building is the competence and the willingness to use it:

**Nice → Capable → Dangerous → Serene about being dangerous**

The last step is the Avasarala quality specifically. Making peace with what you are and what you'll do when necessary.

## What Actually Changes

This arc is **not** a corruption arc or a "she got darker" arc.

Mira's conservatism comes from a need for *legibility* — knowing what things are and how to be in relation to them. The inquisitor path is a natural extension: when diplomacy fails to make something legible, you use authority instead. **Same instinct. Sharper teeth.**

She stops being the person who smooths things over and starts being the person who *decides* when smoothing is warranted and when it isn't.

## Mechanical Direction

### What to invest in:
- **Manipulation** — the core social lever
- **Leadership** — directing the party as instruments
- **Sovereign** — institutional weight that makes people *want* to give her what she wants
- **Intimidation** (eventually) — the quiet kind
- **Contract** (long-term goal — see below)

### What NOT to do:
Do not personally invest heavily in Interrogation. **Use the party instead.**

Mira becomes the person who:
- Reads a room and tells Asher exactly which angle to work
- Knows when to send Finja and Shameus in versus when to walk in herself
- Uses Grandeur to make people want to talk before anyone has to get rough
- Positions the party so that by the time someone needs to break, it almost happens naturally

The party's rougher members become instruments she learns to play well. The Grandeur scene at the biker club in Session 010 is this working correctly already.

## The Contract Art — Long-Term Goal

Contract is architecturally perfect for this arc. It is not a combat tool. It is not an investigation tool. It is a **framework of accountability** — you don't threaten people, you bind them to their own words and let the Dreaming enforce the consequences.

**Contract is a completely separate Art from Sovereign** and must be developed independently, beginning at Contract 1. It has no Sovereign prerequisite — it simply needs to be invested in on its own track.

### Progression and what each level means narratively:

| Level | Cantrip | Narrative meaning |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Contract 1 | **Done Deal** | She begins formalizing relationships — binding agreements with Glamour; oathbreakers suffer automatic botches at the Dreaming's most ironic timing |
| Contract 2 | **Liar's Bell** | She always *knows* when she has been betrayed — who and when, the moment it happens; also makes tracking the oathbreaker easier |
| Contract 3 | **Castigate** | The moment a contract is broken, she may reflexively strike the oathbreaker with any cantrip she knows — even at a distance |
| Contract 4 | **Casual Contract** | Every conversation with her carries weight people don't realize until too late — even idle boasts and offhand promises can be sanctified |
| Contract 5 | **Sanctified Words** | Weaves blessings directly into oaths — bonus dice, Glamour, Willpower, even Bestowments for those who honor their word |

*Note: Liar's Bell, Castigate, and Casual Contract are permanent modifications — once learned, they enhance all uses of Done Deal automatically. They don't need to be actively invoked.*

Casual Contract in particular is the quietly chilling endpoint ability — someone who can bind casual remarks transforms every social interaction into something with potential teeth.

**Note:** This is a long-term investment requiring its own XP track separate from Sovereign. The arc has runway, which is fine — the destination doesn't need to be reached to make the journey meaningful.

## The Asher Dynamic

**OOC framing:** This is a tension to invite and play with, not resolve. Asher "winning" as the Unseelie troublemaker operating just beneath Mira's nose is good story. The social contract at the table supports letting it run.

**In practice:** Mira mostly doesn't notice. Occasionally rolls her eyes when she does. Does not escalate.

**The irony:** Asher's anti-noble ideology keeps poking at the question of what Mira's nobility actually means. Every time he sidesteps her authority he's implicitly asking: *so what are you going to do about it, Sidhe?* His ideological opposition could end up *accelerating* her into becoming more consciously noble. He would probably hate that if he ever realized it.

**The Inquisitor complication:** As Mira grows into institutional authority, Asher gains *legitimate* grievance — she's becoming exactly the thing he distrusts. Both characters following their natures, gap widening organically.

**Mira's awareness:** Mostly oblivious. Occasionally noticing. The Peacock legacy suggests she files things away for when they actually matter — but this is rare and not a primary mode.

## Trigger Approaches

Three possible ways to set the arc in motion:

**1. Gradual drift — no clean moment**
She keeps reaching for Grandeur and authority because it *works*, and one day looks back and notices she's changed. Low drama, high realism. Consistent with how her conservatism is described as largely unconscious.

**2. A conscious threshold moment**
Something in the Butcher investigation or the Ailil sister mystery forces an explicit choice between diplomatic Mira and inquisitor Mira. More dramatic, gives the GM a hook.

**3. External legitimization**
Someone with institutional authority — Edevan, Iawn ap Fiona — formally *names* what she's becoming and treats it as appropriate. Mira may need that external frame before she fully claims the identity shift consciously.

---

## Notes on Campaign Length

The campaign may not run long enough to reach the full endpoint. **That's fine.** The arc has value at every stage of completion. What matters is the direction of travel, not the destination.

The current sessions already show the pattern emerging organically. She is visibly *becoming* something. That is the interesting part.
