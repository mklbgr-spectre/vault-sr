# Mira Arc — Ongoing Notes

> [!note] OOC / Meta Planning
> Working notes on how to *play* the arc described in [[Mira ARC Plan]]. Focus here is practical and table-ready, not theoretical.

---

## The Core Problem We Were Solving

The arc plan describes where Mira is going. This document is about **how to actually get there at the table** — what does the creeping change look like as concrete, reachable behavior in any given session?

---

## The Behavioral Ladder — First Pass

The goal is an escalating list of small, playable choices that mark where Mira is on the arc at any given moment. Not dramatic shifts. One notch at a time.

### Key Principle: Outward Before Inward

The shift registers with **NPCs and strangers first**. Toward the party, the change right now is mostly internal — less anxiety, more steadiness — but not yet visible as changed behavior toward them. That comes later.

---

## Five Scenarios

Each scenario shows current Mira vs. a slightly-more-authoritarian Mira. The warmth is present in both columns. What changes is what the warmth is *doing*.

### 1. The Interrogation Handoff

*An NPC is hostile and uncooperative. The context is urgent — the group is exposed, time is short, and Asher in the room is a wildcard.*

**Current Mira:** Tries reasonable, feels it fail within a beat or two, and backs off. Hands to Asher partly because she doesn't know what else to do, partly to get out from under the discomfort.

**Slightly-more Mira:** Tries longer — not because she's uncertain, but because she's factoring in the room. She knows what an unchecked Asher looks like and she's not ready to hand that over without exhausting her options. When she does step back, she doesn't leave. She stays present. The handoff happens, but she's watching it, not fleeing it.

---

### 2. Party Wants to Rush; Mira Disagrees

*Shamus and Asher are ready to move immediately. Mira thinks they're missing something.*

**Current Mira:** Explains her reasoning at length, invites consensus, hedges the framing ("I just think maybe..."), and if they push back, probably goes along while voicing a residual concern she doesn't press.

**Slightly-more Mira:** States what she thinks the group should do, once, cleanly. Then asks one question: *"What do we gain from going now that we couldn't gain from an hour's preparation?"* Waits for an actual answer. If there isn't one, she moves forward with her plan and doesn't revisit the discussion.

*Note: This scenario was flagged as the most correct temperature for where she is right now.*

---

### 3. Asher Cuts Across a Productive Conversation

*Asher shifts the energy in a conversation that was going well.*

**Current Mira:** Moves immediately to cover — apologies, faster talking, working hard to recover the lost ground. The flustered quality is visible.

**Slightly-more Mira:** Still covers — that instinct isn't gone yet. But she's a beat slower getting there. Less chasing. The redirect to the NPC is composed rather than urgent, and she doesn't over-explain. The smoothing still happens, but from somewhere steadier.

*Note: The visible change here is toward the NPC, not toward Asher. No comment to him. Not yet.*

---

### 4. A Court NPC Miscalibrates Her Rank

*Someone who doesn't know her treats her as just another field investigator — addresses Shamus first, or speaks to the group rather than to her.*

**Current Mira:** Works graciously around it — introduces herself warmly, finds a way to establish who she is without making the correction feel like a correction, puts the NPC at ease about their mistake.

**Slightly-more Mira:** States her name and house once, correctly, with no social cushioning around it. *"Myrrha ni Eilund."* Lets the correction land. Continues from there as though the rank was always established, because as far as she's concerned it was.

*Note: Flagged as second-most correct temperature alongside scenario 2.*

---

### 5. Finja Does Something Naive in a Courtly Setting

*Finja — genuinely unaware of court dynamics — creates friction with an NPC who cares about propriety.*

**Current Mira:** Moves immediately to smooth it, apologizes somewhere in the vicinity of Finja's behalf, works to reassure the NPC the group isn't as rough as that moment suggested.

**Slightly-more Mira:** Still redirects — but she doesn't apologize for Finja. She addresses the NPC on her own authority: *"What she means—"* as clarification, not damage control. She's not managing a mess, she's translating. The NPC feels that difference even if they couldn't name it.

---

## The Through-Line

**Current Mira reflexively fills the gap** created by difficulty, silence, or social mistake.

**Slightly-more Mira makes the gap** and lets other people decide what to do with it — or holds it herself with more composure.

The behavior often looks almost the same. The *quality* of it is different.
