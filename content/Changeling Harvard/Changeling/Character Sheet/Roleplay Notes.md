# Mira — Actual Play Notes

Observations about how Mira is being played at the table, distinct from written backstory. These emerged organically over the first 9 sessions.

---

## The Noble Thread

Mira is the only Sidhe noble in the party, and this shapes her decisions and reactions in a way that is subtle rather than overt. She is warm and genuinely collegial, but there is a grain to her choices that reflects an instinctive relationship with fae order and propriety. Certain things are simply done correctly or not done at all — not as a power move, but as an assumption so deep she probably doesn't examine it.

This is not political conservatism. It is fae-traditional: an instinctive pull toward how things should be in the Dreaming, expressed through what she objects to, what she defers to, and what she finds reassuring vs. alarming.

**Defining moment:** When two fae investigators were waiting for the group, the rest of the party's instinct was "oh no, the cops." Mira's was "oh good, the cops." Institutional fae authority is legitimate to her and its presence is reassuring. She is *of* that system, not subject to it.

---

## The Internal Hierarchy

Mira came into the chronicle with an implicit ordering something like:
*Seelie noble > Seelie commoner > Unseelie noble > Unseelie commoner*

After the Unseelie court turned out to be considerably more civilized than she had unconsciously expected, this has quietly shifted to something closer to:
*Seelie noble > (Seelie commoner = Unseelie noble) > Unseelie commoner*

This revision is probably not fully conscious. She didn't have a moment of realization — she just treated the Unseelie nobles more like peers than anticipated, and it felt natural. The frame is still fundamentally about rank; she extended the benefit of the doubt *upward* to Unseelie nobility rather than dissolving the hierarchy.

This could snap back hard if she encounters genuinely unsavory Unseelie nobles — and when it does, it may land harder than where she started.

---

## Asher

Asher declared distrust of nobles from the start and has only entered 1-on-1 oaths rather than a group oath, despite Mira being consistently helpful and courteous. The tension is ideological on Asher's side — it is not really about what Mira has done, which means she cannot earn her way out of it through behavior alone.

The additional friction: Mira has pushed back on some of Asher's cruder plans and suggestions. From Mira's perspective this is a values and propriety objection she would make toward anyone. From Asher's perspective it probably confirms the original suspicion — "noble thinks my methods are beneath them."

Mira is currently staying warm and patient. This has limits, and Asher keeps adding fuel.

---

## The Party Dynamic

The GM occasionally routes things through Mira alone — a court leader speaking only to her, for instance. This is not just rank acknowledgment; she is genuinely the only one in the group who is both fluent in courtly dynamics and paying that kind of attention.

- **Shamus** — Seelie commoner, plays it consciously: loves the non-courtly world, something of a hooligan, probably the closest to Unseelie if it's a spectrum. Deliberately outside the structures Mira operates in.
- **Finja** — Seelie commoner, naive about court dynamics. Doesn't register the same things Mira registers, not out of opposition but simply unawareness.
- **Thea** — Kith that fits a very unworldly, "weltfremd" quality, played accordingly by a player who brings that same quality naturally. Operates on a genuine fae frequency that has no courtly scaffolding at all.

---

## Mira and Thea

From Mira's unconscious conservative lens, Thea is by far the easiest party member to get along with. There is nothing to navigate — no positioning, no agenda, no opposition. Just someone who exists in a very genuine fae way that Mira can appreciate without friction.

There is likely a quiet protective instinct at play, especially sharing living space. Someone that unworldly needs someone keeping half an eye out, and Mira does this without making it a thing.

This also clarifies something about where Mira's conservatism actually comes from. It is not about control or enforcing hierarchy. It is about *legibility* — knowing what something is and how to be in relation to it. Shamus and Asher are legible but oppositional. Thea is outside every structure Mira knows and somehow produces no friction at all. The conservatism is a framework for navigating, not a need to dominate.
