### **Mira Vogel / Myrrha ni Eilund – Canon Summary**

**Mortal Identity**

- **Name:** Mira Vogel
    
- **Age:** 25
    
- **Date of Birth:** January 1, 2000
    
- **Place of Birth:** Seattle, Capitol Hill district
    
- **Occupation:** Graduate student at Harvard & Teaching Assistant
    
- **Languages:** Fluent in English and German, proficient in French
    
- **Appearance:** Tall, blue-eyed woman with dirty blonde hair, worn long and natural with a slight wave. Wears academic, modest outfits (blouses, slacks, modest dresses), often accented with subtle occult or mythological iconography. Wears glasses due to a vision-related flaw, in both mortal and fae forms. Her posture and expression often radiate warmth and attentiveness, with a nurturing smile that puts people at ease.
    

**Personality & Role**

- Bright, inquisitive, and quietly warm, Mira presents as a calm, empathetic presence. She is idealistic and supportive, prone to fostering trust and hope in others, but still carries a quiet melancholy rooted in vague dreams of betrayal and loss from her fae past. She is highly academically focused and self-disciplined, and although introverted, she makes a sincere effort to be socially kind.
    
- In her group, she often serves as the emotional compass, subtle motivator, and scholarly advisor — a steady anchor who rarely imposes but often enlightens. Think of her as the person who finds the bigger picture or offers the gentle nudge toward the next step.
    

---

### **Backstory**

- Raised in Seattle by German-American parents, Mira had a happy but somewhat isolated childhood. She was always “a little off”—bookish, fascinated by myth, and somewhat mystically inclined. Her German was weak growing up, despite cultural ties to her maternal grandparents.
    
- Her early years at Harvard were strong academically, but in her third year, she began to struggle — distracted, haunted by strange dreams, emotional confusion, and faint sensory anomalies that defied explanation.
    
- A former professor-turned-ally (using the Protégé background) arranged a year-long academic leave disguised as a study opportunity in Germany, with her grandparents acting as a grounding influence.
    
- While abroad, her Chrysalis occurred not in a castle, but an abandoned barn on her grandparents' property. One night, drawn by strange sensations and compulsions, she encountered a creature from German folklore — something that should have been dangerous, but triggered an explosion of Glamour instead. Local fae intervened, rescuing her and introducing her to the Dreaming.
    
- Mira spent her fostering in Germany, coming to terms with her dual nature and undergoing a year-and-a-day of guidance under her fae identity, Myrrha. Her fae past remains vague — only shadowy fragments persist, such as faint feelings of betrayal tied to an Ailil noble and a lost friendship.
    
- Upon returning to Boston, she resumed her studies and took on a TA role secured through her mentor, [[Count Edevan ap Eiluned]] — a stern and enigmatic Sidhe magister whose mortal identity is Dr. Evan C. Albright, a professor emeritus with ties to the Crimson Spire, a hidden bastion of fae academia.

### **Dreamers**

- **Dreamer 1:** Freya Halvorsen, a Comparative Literature freshman at Harvard who sees Mira as a mentor and big-sister figure. Brilliant but burning out; generates Glamour through her writing and her aspiration toward wonder.
    
- **Dreamer 2:** Erik Mahoney, a late-20s MIT student in systems engineering, recovering from burnout. Mira serves as a kind of informal sponsor or mental/emotional support person. He runs a D&D campaign and invited Mira to join — she accepted, forming a quiet, offbeat friendship.

### **Fae Identity**

- **Fae Name:** Myrrha
    
- **Kith & House:** Sidhe (Autumn), House Eiluned
    
- **Court:** Seelie
    
- **Fae Appearance:** Subtly luminous, elfin features that resemble a younger Galadriel. Her beauty has a distant, melancholic edge, like something remembered from a dream. She retains her glasses.
    
- **Core Values:** Hope, harmony, the transformative power of belief and knowledge. Deeply believes in helping others rediscover their passions. She has an idealized vision of the Dreaming, though it is tempered by her faint memories of fae politics and betrayal.
